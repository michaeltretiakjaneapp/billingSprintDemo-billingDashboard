Patient Billing (Collecting the Patient Portion)

After the insurer pays (or for self-pay patients), this journey covers how the clinic determines what the patient owes, generates a statement or invoice, collects the payment, and reconciles the visit as fully paid. Includes the "Payable to Patient" vs "Collect from Insurer" mode split, balance write-off and adjustment workflows, and the patient dispute path. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #support_us_manualtherapy, and #pt-insurance-coordinate.

G1 Patient receives a clear, accurate statement immediately after the insurer's ERA is processed — no manual trigger required
G2 Clinic collects the patient portion without a phone call or manual follow-up in the majority of cases
G3 Visit is fully reconciled (zero balance) with a complete audit trail of insurer payment + patient payment
0
Determine Patient Balance
Step 0.1
Jane calculates the remaining patient balance after insurer payment is applied
Jane System
Task
Apply insurer EOB payments to the invoice and surface the remaining patient balance
▶
Pain Point
Jane calculates the remaining balance based on what was entered in the EOB — but if the biller entered the wrong allowed amount, adjustment, or patient responsibility split during ERA processing, the resulting patient balance is also wrong. There is no reconciliation check between what the ERA said and what was entered.
Suggested Opportunity
A post-ERA summary showing "Insurer paid: $X | Patient responsibility: $Y | Remaining: $Z" before the statement is generated would let the biller spot errors before they reach the patient.
Biller
Task
Verify copay, deductible, and coinsurance amounts are correct before billing the patient
▶
Pain Point
If eligibility was not verified before the appointment, the biller may not know the patient's actual deductible status — so the amount billed to the patient can be wrong. This is only discovered after the ERA arrives, sometimes weeks later, requiring a corrected statement and eroding patient trust.
Community"Patient was billed the wrong copay amount because we didn't verify eligibility ahead of time. Now the patient is upset and we have to chase them for the difference." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
Real-time eligibility verification (Journey #4) feeds the deductible and copay into the patient's policy in Jane — so when the ERA arrives, the expected patient balance is already set and the post-ERA math is a confirmation, not a discovery.
Step 0.2
Biller determines whether this is "Collect from Patient" or "Payable to Patient" mode
Biller
Task
Identify whether the insurer pays the clinic directly or pays the patient (assignment of benefits)
▶
Pain Point
"Payable to Patient" mode in Jane — where the insurer reimburses the patient directly instead of the clinic — is a frequently misunderstood setting. Billers often don't know this mode exists, apply it inconsistently, or switch modes after the fact, which can create accounting errors and misaligned balances.
Community"I'm confused about when to use Payable to Patient vs the normal mode. The insurer sent the cheque to the patient but we still need to collect our portion from the patient — how do I set this up correctly?" — Support thread, #support_us_manualtherapy
Suggested Pain
When a patient pays out of pocket at the time of service and the insurer later reimburses the patient (not the clinic), the clinic's invoice is already marked as paid — but technically the clinic collected the full amount from the patient, not the insurer. Jane's accounting for this scenario is unclear and varies by how the biller recorded the initial payment.
Suggested Opportunity
In-context guidance on the invoice view — explaining Payable to Patient vs Collect from Insurer with a real example — would reduce support tickets and biller errors significantly. A prompt at ERA processing time ("This ERA response is for a patient-assigned claim — switch to Payable to Patient mode?") would catch it at the right moment.
1
Generate & Send Patient Statement
Step 1.1
Biller generates a patient invoice or statement and sends it
Biller
Task
Navigate to the patient's invoice, review the balance, and send the invoice via email or print
▶
Pain Point
Sending a patient statement is a fully manual step — the biller must navigate to each patient's invoice individually after ERA processing. There is no automated trigger to send a statement once the ERA is marked reviewed and the patient balance is calculated. High-volume clinics must batch-send statements manually, which is time-consuming.
Community"We have to manually go into each patient record after processing ERAs to send invoices. There's no way to batch-send statements to all patients with outstanding balances after a remittance is done." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
An auto-send rule — "after ERA is marked reviewed, automatically send statement to any patient whose balance changed" — would eliminate the manual dispatch step for routine cases and ensure patients receive timely bills without biller intervention.
Jane System
Task
Generate the patient-facing invoice showing service date, codes, insurer payment, and balance owing
▶
Pain Point
Patient invoices in Jane display raw billing and diagnosis codes (e.g. "97110 — Therapeutic Exercise" or "M54.5") that most patients do not understand. There is no plain-language description of what was billed. Patients frequently call the clinic asking what the invoice is for, creating unnecessary admin work.
Community"Patients call us all the time asking what the codes on their invoice mean. It would be great if Jane could show something like 'Physical Therapy — 30 minutes' instead of just the CPT code." — Canny feature request, Patient Billing
Pain Point
The patient invoice does not clearly show the full payment breakdown — what the insurer paid, what was written off as a contractual adjustment, and what the patient owes. Patients see only the final balance, which leads to confusion and disputes when they expected insurance to cover more.
Suggested Opportunity
A patient-facing invoice summary: "Billed: $150 | Insurance paid: $90 | Contractual adjustment: $30 | Your balance: $30" — with plain-language service descriptions — would reduce patient confusion and disputes significantly.
Step 1.2
Patient receives the statement and decides how to pay
Patient
Task
Open the invoice email or statement and understand what is owed and why
▶
Pain Point
The invoice email from Jane lacks context — patients receive a dollar amount with minimal explanation of the service, what insurance covered, or why their portion is what it is. Many patients don't pay simply because they don't understand the bill, not because they're unwilling.
Feedback"Patients often ignore invoices not because they can't pay, but because they don't understand why they owe money when they thought insurance covered everything." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A "why do I owe this?" explainer embedded in the patient-facing invoice — showing a plain-language breakdown of the insurer's decision — would reduce confusion-driven non-payment and support calls.
Patient
Task
Choose payment method: online portal, in-person at front desk, or mail cheque
▶
Pain Point
Not all patients use email or the online portal — some patients, particularly older demographics, prefer to pay by cheque or in person. Jane's patient billing workflow is optimised for online/digital payment; clinics with mixed patient populations must manage payment tracking across multiple channels manually.
Community"We have a lot of older patients who mail us a cheque. There's no good way to track that in Jane — we record it manually and hope nothing slips through." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
2
Collect Payment
Step 2.1
Patient pays online via Jane's patient portal or at the front desk
Patient
Task
Click payment link in invoice email → enter card details → submit payment
▶
Pain Point
The patient invoice link in Jane expires or becomes inaccessible if the invoice status changes (e.g. partial payment recorded, biller edits the invoice). Patients who click a stale link receive an error or see a different amount than expected, leading to support calls.
Community"Patient says the link I sent doesn't work anymore. I updated the invoice after sending it and now the original link is dead." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Invoice links should resolve to the current state of the invoice, not a snapshot — so even if the biller updates the balance, the patient's link remains valid and reflects the corrected amount.
Clinic Admin
Task
Collect copay or outstanding balance at check-in or check-out via Jane's POS/payment screen
▶
Pain Point
When collecting a copay at check-in, the front desk may not yet know the patient's exact copay amount — especially if the insurer's response for previous visits hasn't been processed yet. They either guess (using the eligibility-verified copay amount), skip it and bill later, or ask the patient to wait while they look it up.
Community"We try to collect the copay at check-in but half the time we don't actually know the right amount because the ERA from last week hasn't been processed. It's a guessing game." — Billing ride-along notes
Opportunity
Eligibility verification (Journey #4) surfacing the confirmed copay on the appointment panel — visible to the front desk at check-in — means the admin can confidently collect the correct amount without leaving the scheduling view.
Step 2.2
Jane records the patient payment and updates the invoice balance
Jane System
Task
Apply the patient payment to the invoice and update the outstanding balance
▶
Pain Point
If a patient pays a partial amount (e.g. $20 of a $50 balance), Jane does not track partial payment status clearly — the invoice either shows as fully unpaid or requires manual intervention to reflect the partial. There is no "partially paid" state with a remaining balance tracker visible at a glance.
Community"Patient paid half their bill but I can't easily see which patients have partial balances remaining — I have to open each invoice individually to check." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A "Partially Paid" invoice status with the remaining balance prominently displayed — and a filterable view of all partially paid invoices — would give billers a clear action queue for patient balance follow-up.
Biller
Task
Follow up on unpaid patient balances that haven't been paid after the statement was sent
▶
Pain Point
There is no automated patient balance follow-up in Jane — no second reminder email, no overdue notice, no escalation path. Billers must manually identify aging patient balances (by exporting the billing report), then individually contact each patient. Most clinics don't do this systematically, leaving money on the table.
Community"We know there are outstanding patient balances sitting there but we don't have time to manually chase each one. If Jane sent a reminder automatically after 30 days that would save us hours every month." — Canny feature request, Patient Billing
Opportunity
Automated patient balance reminders — configurable (e.g. send a reminder after 14 days, a second after 30 days, escalate after 60 days) — would dramatically increase collection rates without additional biller effort. This is a well-understood pattern in practice management software.
3
Reconcile & Close the Visit
Step 3.1
Biller confirms the invoice is fully paid and the visit is reconciled
Biller
Task
Verify insurer payment + patient payment = full billed amount (minus contractual adjustments)
▶
Pain Point
There is no single reconciliation view in Jane that shows: billed amount, insurer payment, contractual adjustment, patient payment, and remaining balance — all in one place for a given visit. Billers must mentally assemble this from multiple screens (invoice, ERA response, payment record), which is error-prone and slow.
Community"To check if a visit is truly 'done' I have to look at the invoice, the ERA response, and the payment record separately. I always worry I'm missing something." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A per-visit financial summary card — "Billed: $X | Insurance paid: $X | Patient paid: $X | Adjustment: $X | Balance: $0 ✓" — visible directly on the appointment or patient profile would give billers instant reconciliation confirmation.
Biller
Task
Write off any remaining small balances (rounding errors, minimum balance thresholds, contractual adjustments)
▶
Pain Point
Writing off a small remaining balance (e.g. a $0.01 rounding difference or a $2 contractual adjustment) requires navigating through multiple steps in Jane with no clear "write off" action button. Billers develop workarounds — manually recording a $0.01 patient payment, or leaving the invoice open indefinitely — which distorts the books.
Community"I have dozens of invoices with $0.01 remaining because I can't figure out how to write off a balance that small without recording a fake payment. It pollutes my outstanding balances list." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
A write-off action on invoices — with a reason code (contractual adjustment, bad debt, courtesy write-off, below minimum balance threshold) and an audit trail — would make balance reconciliation clean and give clinics proper accounting records for each visit.
Step 3.2
Invoice marked as fully paid — visit is closed
Jane System
Task
Mark invoice as Paid and remove it from the outstanding balance queue
▶
Pain Point
Invoices that are technically "zero balance" but not explicitly marked Paid continue to appear in billing reports and outstanding balance views in some contexts. Billers are never fully sure whether an invoice is truly closed or just temporarily at zero.
Community"My report still shows this invoice as outstanding even though the balance is $0. I don't understand why it's still showing up." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-mark an invoice as Paid when the running balance reaches $0 — with a clear visual state change — so billers have unambiguous confirmation that the visit is fully reconciled.
4
Handle Exceptions
Step 4.1
Patient disputes the bill or questions the amount
Patient
Task
Patient contacts the clinic to dispute or question their bill
▶
Pain Point
When a patient calls to dispute a bill, the admin or biller must navigate across Jane screens (invoice, ERA response, eligibility check, appointment notes) to piece together a full explanation. There is no single "billing history" view that tells the story of a visit from service to final payment in chronological order.
Community"Patient is on the phone asking why they owe $45. I have to look at three different screens to explain what their insurance covered and what they owe — by which time I've already lost them." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A visit-level billing timeline — "Service → Claim submitted → ERA received → Insurance paid $X → Patient balance $Y → Statement sent → Patient paid $Z" — would give any staff member a full picture of the financial story of a visit in seconds, enabling faster and more confident patient communication.
Biller
Task
Investigate the dispute, correct any errors, re-issue the statement or process a refund if warranted
▶
Pain Point
If a billing error is found (wrong code, wrong insurer, wrong copay amount), the biller must re-open the claim, make corrections, potentially resubmit, and then re-generate the patient statement. There is no guided "billing correction" path — each error type requires a different sequence of steps that billers must know from experience.
Community"Every billing correction is a different puzzle. Wrong insurer is one set of steps, wrong code is another. I wish Jane had a correction flow that walked me through what to fix." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A "correct this bill" workflow that guides the biller through the appropriate steps based on the type of error — wrong insurer, wrong code, wrong patient, overpayment — would reduce correction errors and training overhead.
Step 4.2
Self-pay or uninsured patient — no insurer in the loop
Biller
Task
Generate and send patient invoice for the full service amount (no ERA step, no insurer payment)
▶
Pain Point
For self-pay patients, the billing flow is simpler but Jane doesn't clearly distinguish self-pay billing from insured billing in the workflow. Billers sometimes accidentally route a self-pay invoice through the claim submission flow or set it to "awaiting insurer response" — putting it in a limbo state.
Community"I accidentally submitted a claim for a self-pay patient. Now it's sitting in Submitted and I can't figure out how to pull it back without voiding the whole invoice." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A clear "Self-Pay" invoice mode that bypasses the claim submission workflow entirely — with a direct path to patient billing — would prevent self-pay invoices from accidentally entering the insurance billing queue.
Biller
Task
Apply a sliding scale, courtesy discount, or financial hardship reduction to the invoice
▶
Pain Point
Applying a discount or sliding scale fee to a self-pay invoice requires a manual adjustment in Jane with no pre-configured discount types — every adjustment is a one-off. Clinics that have formal sliding scale policies (e.g. income-based tiers) must track these outside Jane and manually apply the right amount each time.
Community"We have a sliding scale for low-income patients but there's no way to set that up in Jane. I manually calculate the right amount and type in a custom discount every single time." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Configurable discount types (sliding scale tiers, courtesy adjustments, hardship write-offs) that can be applied from a dropdown on the invoice — with audit trail and reason codes — would support clinics with formal financial assistance programs.
