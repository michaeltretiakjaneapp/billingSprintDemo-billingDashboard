End-of-Year Policy Reset

How a clinic prepares its insurance policies for January 1 — identifying which patients need policy resets, resetting deductibles and visit counts, linking new policy versions to already-scheduled appointments, and handling the fallout when the reset is missed or done late. One of the most universally dreaded annual tasks in insurance billing. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #support_ca_manualtherapy_toques, #jane_community_posts_fb, #product-insights, #hot_seat_support, and direct biller interviews.

G1 Every patient's deductible, visit count, and copay are correctly zeroed/updated before January 1 without visiting each record individually
G2 Already-scheduled January appointments automatically reflect the new policy — no appointment-by-appointment manual update
G3 Clinics that miss the reset deadline have a clear recovery path that doesn't require re-doing all affected appointments
0
Prepare Before Year-End (December)
Step 0.1
Identify all active patients with insurance policies that need to be reset for the new year
Biller
Task
Identify every patient with an active insurance policy — there is no built-in "reset checklist" in Jane
▶
Pain Point
There is no Jane-native list of "policies that need to be reset for the new year." Billers must either know their patient list from memory or manually review the insurer pages patient by patient to identify who has an active policy with deductibles or visit counts that will reset January 1
Feedback"I despite January. I literally have to check every single person." — Biller interview, quoted in #product-insights and #design
Suggested Opportunity
A year-end policy reset checklist — surfaced automatically in December as a Jane task — listing every patient with an active insurance policy and their current deductible / visit count values, with a bulk-reset action, would transform a multi-day manual task into a single guided workflow.
Biller
Task
Pro-active workaround: create new "2026 policy" records ahead of time, attach them to pre-booked recurring appointments
▶
Opportunity
The most advanced workaround billers have discovered is pre-creating 2026 policy records in December and manually swapping them onto recurring future appointments — treating policy records like versioned documents. This is an effective pattern but entirely user-discovered; Jane doesn't guide billers toward it.
Community"Katie Rau shared that her clinic has started creating the new policies ahead of time (e.g., '2026 insurance') and uses those for next year's recurring appointments." — Community digest, #jane_community_posts_fb
1
Reset Policies in Jane
Step 1.1
Navigate to Insurer → Insurance Policies and use "Reset for 2026" on each patient policy
Biller
Task
Go to Billing → Insurers → select insurer → Insurance Policies → select patient policies → "Reset for 2026"
▶
Opportunity
The "Reset for [year]" feature — accessible from the insurer's policy list — does exist and creates a new versioned policy record for the new year, keeping 2025 claims associated with the 2025 policy. It can also be done in bulk by selecting multiple patients at once on the insurer page.
Pain Point
The "Reset for year" feature is insurer-scoped — you can only bulk-reset policies for one insurer at a time. A clinic with patients on multiple insurers (Aetna, BCBS, Medicare, United) must repeat this workflow for every insurer separately. There is no cross-insurer bulk reset
Community"They could reset the secondary first and then reset Medicare. They would just need to ensure that the reset policies are linked to 2026 appointments." — Lee-Anne, #hot_seat_support
Biller
Task
Manually zero out deductible amounts for each patient whose deductible resets — separate from the policy reset
▶
Pain Point
Resetting the policy creates a new policy version but does not automatically zero the deductible amount to $0 — that is a separate manual step for each patient. Billers must remember to also change the deductible to zero and add a note, then remember to reset it again at the start of each new year
Feedback"Change all deductibles to zero. Make a note on it. Then I have to remember in the beginning of the year to reset everybody. In order for Jane to recognize that the person doesn't owe anything, I would have to set deductibles to zero on each appointment, so I just do it at the insurance level." — Biller interview, #product-insights
Suggested Opportunity
As part of the "Reset for year" flow, Jane could prompt: "Would you like to reset the deductible to $0 for this patient's new policy? This is correct for most plans that reset January 1." — making the deductible zeroing part of the reset action rather than a separate remembered step.
Step 1.2
Reset visit counts — if the clinic tracks them manually in the policy
Biller
Task
Reset the visit count field on each policy to reflect the new plan year's allowed visits
▶
Opportunity
The policy reset does carry over the visit count reset as part of the new versioned policy — this is one area where the existing reset flow does provide meaningful continuity. The 2025 policy retains its visit count history; the new 2026 policy starts fresh.
Pain Point
Visit counts in Jane policies are manually maintained — Jane does not track visits consumed against a policy visit limit automatically. If a biller forgets to update the visit count or enters it incorrectly, there's no system safeguard. The only way to catch this is a manual audit.
2
Link New Policy to Already-Scheduled January Appointments
Step 2.1
New policy does NOT automatically attach to future appointments — biller must update each one
Biller
Task
For every pre-booked January appointment: remove the old policy, re-add the new 2026 policy
▶
Pain Point
After resetting a policy, every already-scheduled appointment still holds the old 2025 policy. Jane does not propagate the new policy to future appointments — the biller must manually remove and re-add the insurance on each upcoming visit. For clinics with large recurring appointment books this is a multi-day task
Community"Sonya Olsen raised that when a patient's insurance changes, Jane doesn't automatically update future appointments. For clinics with many pre-booked appointments, changing each one manually is very time-consuming. She suggested it would be nice if there were an option to 'update all future appointments.'" — Community digest, #jane_community_posts_fb
Opportunity
A "propagate to future appointments" option — offered at the point of policy reset — asking "This patient has X appointments after January 1. Would you like to attach the new policy to all of them?" would eliminate the biggest labour cost of the annual reset and is the most-requested feature in this space.
Biller
Task
Confirm that updating copay on the policy also doesn't propagate to scheduled appointments
▶
Pain Point
This is not limited to year-end — any time a copay or coverage amount is changed on an existing policy, Jane does not ask if the change should apply to already-scheduled appointments. Billers must either update scheduled appointments manually or remove/re-add the policy on each one. This is explicitly on the Insurance Foundations design debt backlog
Community"If I change the co-pay, it doesn't ask me if I want to apply to next appointments and it should, because then I have to do it manually." — Biller interview, #product-insights (confirmed on design debt backlog by John Whaley)
Suggested Opportunity
A "schedule this change" option — letting billers set an effective date for policy changes so they take effect automatically on future appointments booked on or after that date — would solve both the year-end reset propagation problem and the mid-year copay update problem in one feature.
3
Recovery: When the Reset Is Missed or Done Late
Step 3.1
Clinic submits January claims on the old 2025 policy — deductibles and counts are wrong on all claims
Biller
Task
Discover the problem when deductible amounts don't match ERA responses in January
▶
Pain Point
When a clinic misses the reset, January claims are submitted on the old policy with end-of-year accumulated deductibles. The ERA comes back showing the full new-year deductible — but the EOB in Jane is pre-filled with the old carryover deductible — and the biller must manually correct every EOB to match the ERA
Community"They missed this step and had to go back and correct everything. They are now finding that the deductible and allowed amount are not populating correctly and they have to update their EOBs manually." — Chelsea, #support_us_medical_trailblazers
Suggested Opportunity
An early-January alert — "It looks like some of your patients' policies haven't been reset yet for the new year. Would you like to reset them now?" — would catch clinics that miss the December window before they submit a large batch of incorrectly-configured January claims.
Biller
Task
Reset the policy now — but the deductible on already-arrived appointments must still be manually corrected
▶
Pain Point
Even after a late reset, arrived appointments that were processed on the old policy retain the old deductible value. Removing and re-adding the insurance policy on an arrived appointment is necessary but still doesn't automatically update the deductible value in the claim — the biller must also update the EOB manually for each affected visit
Community"They've removed the deductible from the policy but re-adding the policy to the appointment still reflects the deductible from before it was removed. Is removing the whole policy and then re-adding it still the only way to resolve this? That seems like a nuclear option." — Jordan, #support_usa_billing
Step 3.2
January deductible resets cause confusing EOB discrepancies — patient and clinic both confused
Clinic Admin
Task
Explain to patients why their January invoice looks different — deductible reset means they owe more than usual
▶
Pain Point
Every January, clinics receive a wave of patient confusion about why their covered amount dropped. The deductible reset means patients who had a fully-met deductible in December suddenly owe their full deductible again. Deductibles don't display prominently in patient communications from Jane, leaving the front desk to explain this reactively
Community"With January comes insurance resets, and many insurers apply a deductible at this time of year. The amount shown as 'covered' has the patient's deductible subtracted from it, which can make the patient portion look a bit funky." — Liz, #support_ca_manualtherapy_toques
Suggested Opportunity
A patient-facing message or appointment confirmation note — "Note: your insurance deductible resets January 1. Your patient responsibility for this visit may be higher than usual until your deductible is met." — would proactively set expectations and reduce front-desk explanation calls in the first two months of every year.
4
The Broader Design Gap — Scheduled Policy Changes
Step 4.1
Jane has no mechanism to schedule a policy change to take effect on a future date
Biller
Task
Attempt to schedule a copay rate change to take effect January 1 — must be done manually on the day
▶
Pain Point
There is no way in Jane to schedule a policy change to take effect on a future date — a clinic wanting to update a CPT rate or copay for the new year must make the change on exactly January 1 or accept that early-January appointments will be wrong. This is a known product gap explicitly flagged by John Whaley
Community"One thing Jane doesn't do very well today is help clinics with these future-oriented updates — things like wanting to update CPT rates, or change the default copay for a patient policy. A clinic might want to update their rates and have that take effect on a certain date, but there's no way to 'schedule' that in Jane." — John Whaley, #pt-insurance-setup
Opportunity
Scheduled (future-dated) policy changes — set in advance, taking effect automatically on a specified date — would solve the year-end reset entirely for most clinics: set the new copay and deductible in December, effective January 1, and let Jane handle the rest. This is the complete solution to the problem rather than the current workaround-based approach.
