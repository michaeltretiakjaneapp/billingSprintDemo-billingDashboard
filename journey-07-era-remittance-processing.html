ERA / Remittance Processing

How a biller processes an Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA) — opening the ERA, recording the payment, reviewing individual claim responses, applying EOB adjustments, marking responses reviewed, and handling the complex edge cases (reversals, neutral reversals, consumer spending accounts, linked / unlinked responses). Includes the Remi hackathon AI automation project. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #ai-sdk-hack-assistive-remittances, #pt-insurance-coordinate, and #proj-insurance-ai.

G1 95% of routine ERA claims are processed in a single pass without opening each one individually
G2 Exceptions (denials, reversals, mislinked responses) are surfaced clearly and actioned without requiring expert-level billing knowledge
G3 PDF and paper EOBs can be processed with the same fidelity as electronic ERAs

ERA Arrives in Jane
Step 0.1
Jane receives 835 ERA from clearinghouse and creates a remittance record
Jane System
Task
Parse 835 file, link responses to matching claim invoices, surface in Remittances folder
▶
Pain Point
Response auto-linking can fail — Jane sometimes links responses to the wrong invoice (e.g. a secondary response linked to the primary claim) or leaves them unlinked entirely, requiring the biller to manually reconnect them before processing can begin
Community"It looks like all of the responses are correctly linked to the right claim in Jane, except this one. It's linked to the secondary, but it should be linked to the primary claim." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A "review linking" step before the ERA is opened for processing — showing which responses were auto-linked and flagging any that couldn't be matched — would catch linking errors before billers invest time processing responses that are attached to the wrong claim.
Biller
Task
Record the ERA payment amount (EFT or check) to unlock EOB editing
▶
Pain Point
Recording the payment is a prerequisite to processing the ERA — but some billers accidentally record an EOB manually before the ERA arrives, creating a conflict between the manual record and the electronic remittance that requires significant cleanup
Community"They Recorded an EOB before the remittance was linked, most likely on accident or with a paper copy of an EOB. Then a remittance came through, and marked it as response received." — Jeff H., #support_us_manualtherapy
Opportunity
The Remi hackathon project auto-records the ERA payment on upload — removing this manual prerequisite step entirely for clinics that use electronic ERAs and want Remi's autonomous processing mode.

Scan ERA for Exceptions
Step 1.1
Biller opens ERA and scans responses to identify which need attention
Biller
Task
Click through every response one by one to assess whether it's routine or exceptional
▶
Pain Point
Processing ERAs in Jane feels repetitive and cognitively taxing — billers must open and review every claim from a batch even when 80–95% are routine and match expected reimbursement. The entire ERA queue requires 100% manual review to find a small minority of exceptions
Community"Processing ERAs in Jane feels repetitive and is cognitively taxing. Billers must open and review every claim from a batch — even when 80–95% are routine and match expected reimbursement. This forces 100% manual review to find a small minority of exceptions." — Mosh, #pt-insurance-coordinate
Opportunity
The Remi AI hackathon project classifies each claim response as "expected" (routine, matches expected payment) or "needs attention" (underpayment, denial, adjustment, reversal). Expected claims can be bulk-approved; only exceptions require individual review. This is the core bet of the Assistive Remittance project.
Feedback"95% of cases claims are expected, and finding the anomalies is like finding the needle in a haystack. Our solution automates the routine and surfaces the exceptions." — Mosh, #ai-sdk-hack-assistive-remittances
Biller
Task
Identify reversals, neutral reversals, and takebacks buried within the ERA
▶
Pain Point
Reversals and neutral reversals look nearly identical in Jane — both show as separate response entries. A neutral reversal (insurer reprocessed a claim but paid the same amount) requires no action, but a real reversal (insurer is clawing back a prior payment) requires urgent action. The distinction is not surfaced clearly
Community"She received a remittance with SO many 'reversal of previous payments' and EOBs that had previously been processed, and it's all quite intimidating to handle. We want to make sure everything's recorded accurately." — Rebecca, #support_us_manualtherapy_coyotes
Suggested Opportunity
A visual distinction between "neutral reversal — no action needed" and "active reversal — payment was clawed back" in the ERA response list would eliminate one of the most confusing and anxiety-inducing scenarios in remittance processing.

Process Routine Claims (The 95%)
Step 2.1
Apply payment and mark response as Reviewed for expected claims
Biller
Task
Open each routine response, verify amounts match, click Apply Changes to mark as Reviewed
▶
Pain Point
Even when no changes need to be made to an EOB, billers must still open each response individually and click "Apply Changes" to move it out of Response Received — there's no way to mark a batch of routine responses as reviewed in a single action
Community"After recording a payment and reviewing responses, is it necessary to click 'Apply Changes' even if no changes were made to an EOB? The payment has already been recorded, the status is 'reviewed' but the claim still shows up in Response Received." — Anika, #support_saturday_crew
Opportunity
Bulk actions for routine responses — "Mark all expected as Reviewed" — are actively in development as part of the Assistive Remittance project (Elnaz, #pt-insurance-coordinate). This is the most direct non-AI improvement to ERA processing speed.
Biller
Task
Collect patient responsibility — copay, deductible, coinsurance — while the ERA is still open
▶
Pain Point
Billers who want to collect patient responsibility while reviewing the ERA must either work in multiple tabs or fully complete the entire ERA before switching to patient billing — there's no patient responsibility tab within the remittance review window
Community"It would be great to have a Client Responsibility tab within the remittance review window. Currently, clinics need to either work out of multiple tabs or fully review a remittance before going to collect client responsibility." — Customer FR, #customer_feature_requests
Opportunity
A patient responsibility tab embedded within the ERA review panel — showing the outstanding patient balance for each claim in the ERA — would let billers collect copays and deductibles in the same flow as processing the insurer response.
3
Handle Exception Claims
Step 3.1
Biller opens an exception response and interprets the EOB adjustment
Biller
Task
Understand what the insurer paid, denied, wrote off, and what the patient owes
▶
Pain Point
The EOB adjustment logic for secondary claims is especially confusing — the "Paid Amount" column in Jane pulls from the full allowed amount, not the actual secondary payment, requiring the biller to mentally calculate the real secondary payment by subtracting the primary amount from what's shown
Community"The ERA reads: -$149.72 OA 23. The Paid Amount column is reflecting the full allowed amount, but they're taking into consideration the primary paid amount $149.72, so the secondary paid amount is actually just the $30.00." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Show the net secondary payment (after primary coordination) as a distinct field in the EOB rather than only the allowed amount — reducing the mental arithmetic billers currently perform on every secondary claim in an ERA.
Biller
Task
Decide: adjust the EOB, adjust the allowed amount, or collect from patient / next payer
▶
Pain Point
The "Allowed Amounts" decision — Leave Outstanding, Adjust By, Collect from Next Payer, Collect from Patient — has no guidance in the UI. New billers regularly select the wrong option, creating reconciliation problems that take significant support effort to untangle
Community"She can select 'collect from next payer' in the paid amount column, so that the $100 moves to payer #2. Then, when she receives that remittance, if it's not covered, she can adjust the EOB again to 'collect from patient' so the charge finally moves to the patient." — Jason, #hot_seat_support
Opportunity
Contextual guidance on the Allowed Amounts field — "If this amount should be written off, choose Adjust By. If the patient owes this, choose Collect from Patient" — would dramatically reduce the most common category of EOB processing errors among new billers.
Step 3.2
Biller edits the EOB and applies changes
Biller
Task
Edit the paid amount, adjust the allowed amounts, and click Apply Changes
▶
Pain Point
A locked $0 billing code on an invoice can block the EOB from being edited — the code is locked because it has no fee, but the ERA is reporting it with a non-zero amount. The biller must first go to the patient profile, allow editing, remove and re-add the code, then return to the ERA to unlock editing
Community"The code 99212 on the ERA is $158 billed amount and the EOB shows the code as $0. This code is locked, due to a $0 billing code." — Katie, #support_us_medical_trailblazers
Step 3.3
Biller handles paper / PDF EOBs with no electronic ERA
Biller
Task
Manually transcribe payment amounts from paper EOB into Jane
▶
Pain Point
Some insurers still pay by check and send paper EOBs — the entire remittance must be manually entered into Jane with no electronic format to parse. This is especially painful for Billing Service teams managing multiple clinics
Community"A pain point we have is manually posting payments we get only as PDFs. Is it possible to convert a PDF to an 835?" — Karen, #proj-insurance-ai
Opportunity
PDF-to-835 conversion — or AI-assisted OCR extraction of payment fields from a PDF EOB into Jane's EOB entry form — would bring paper EOB processing closer to parity with electronic ERA processing.
4
Remi: AI-Assisted Remittance (Hackathon Project)
Step 4.1
Remi agent classifies each claim response as "expected" or "needs attention"
Jane System
Task
Run claim response against configurable rules (Roux): allowed amounts, CARC codes, patient responsibility match
▶
Opportunity
The Remi hackathon build (Elnaz, Warunika, Lukas, farmerchuk, Jan) classifies each response using rules: a claim is "expected" if all codes have a positive non-zero allowed amount, only standard CARC codes (PR-1, PR-2, PR-3, CO-45) are present, patient responsibility matches the invoiced amount, and no prior EOB was recorded. All other cases are flagged for biller review.
Jane System
Task
Auto-apply payment and mark routine responses as Reviewed (in autonomous mode)
▶
Opportunity
In the hackathon Remi prototype, expected claims have their EOB auto-applied and are marked Reviewed without biller intervention — with a settings toggle to enable/disable autonomous mode. Exceptions are surfaced with highlighted failed rules so billers know exactly what to look at.
Step 4.2
Biller reviews exceptions surfaced by Remi and takes action
Biller
Task
Review the minority of flagged claims — understanding exactly which rule failed and why
▶
Opportunity
The Remi prototype highlights exactly which rule failed for each exception — "allowed amount is $0" or "CARC code OA-18 is not a standard code" — so billers don't have to diagnose the issue from scratch. The exception becomes actionable, not just visible.
Suggested Pain Point
The path from "Remi flagged this" to "I know what to do about it" still requires billing expertise — Remi surfaces the exception but doesn't yet prescribe the resolution. Next-step guidance per exception type remains an open design question as the project moves into H2 planning.
