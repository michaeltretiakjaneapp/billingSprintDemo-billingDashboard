Coordination of Benefits (COB)

How a clinic bills a patient with two or more insurance plans — setting up the correct policy priority order, submitting the primary claim, receiving the primary ERA, passing the remaining balance to secondary, and handling the secondary claim lifecycle. Covers US (electronic secondary claims) and Canadian (significant integration gaps) separately. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #support_ca_insurance, #pt-insurance, #hot_seat_support, and #jane_community_posts_fb.

G1 Staff correctly identify which policy is primary and which is secondary before the first claim is submitted
G2 The secondary claim auto-populates with the primary EOB data so billers don't have to manually transcribe what the primary paid
G3 The remaining patient balance is correctly calculated and communicated after both payers respond
0
Set Up Dual Policies on the Patient Profile
Step 0.1
Identify which policy is primary and which is secondary
Clinic Admin
Task
Determine correct policy priority order (Birthday Rule, Medicare last-payer rules, employer plan order)
▶
Pain Point
Priority order rules are complex — Medicare primary rules, Medicaid last-payer rules, auto-accident liability order, and employer plan rules all apply differently by patient situation. There is no COB priority guidance in Jane; billers must know these rules from external sources
Community"COB logic — ensure correct insurance order (Medicare primary rules, Medicaid last payer, auto accident liability, etc.) — appears to be absolutely missing." — Community feedback, #jane_community_posts_fb
Suggested Opportunity
A COB priority guide surfaced when a second policy is added to a patient profile — with rules of thumb for common scenarios (Medicare, employer plans, Birthday Rule) — would reduce wrong-order submissions and the downstream claim denials they cause.
Clinic Admin
Task
Add both policies to the patient profile and set the correct priority order
▶
Pain Point
Both primary and secondary policies must also be attached at the appointment level — adding policies to the profile is not sufficient. Staff who don't know this skip the appointment-level attachment, causing the secondary claim to never be generated
Community"The secondary insurance policy needs to be added to the appointment. This can be done by manually adding the secondary policy after the primary, or by using the 'Copy previous' option when booking." — Rachel, #support_billing
Suggested Opportunity
When a patient has both primary and secondary policies on file, auto-attach both to new appointments by default — or at minimum show a visible "secondary policy not attached" indicator in the appointment panel before billing starts.
1
Submit Primary Claim & Wait for ERA
Step 1.1
Submit primary claim electronically — secondary claim marked "Waiting on Primary"
Biller
Task
Submit primary claim — Jane auto-creates a secondary claim marked "Waiting on Primary"
▶
Opportunity
Jane generates the secondary claim automatically when the primary is submitted and both policies are on the appointment — this is a genuine strength. The secondary claim holds "Waiting on Primary" status until the primary ERA is received and processed.
Biller
Task
Wait for the primary ERA before touching the secondary claim
▶
Pain Point
Secondary claim auto-population is inconsistent — if the primary claim is still in Draft when a biller looks at the secondary, the secondary opens blank and requires manual entry. Many billers don't know they need to wait for the primary ERA before working the secondary
Community"When a primary claim hasn't been submitted yet, Jane doesn't have confirmed primary-claim data to reliably auto-populate a secondary claim. In some cases it may look like pieces are filling in, but that behavior isn't guaranteed." — Rachel, #support_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Surface a clear "waiting for primary ERA" status on the secondary claim with an estimated timeframe — and lock editing until the primary ERA is received — to prevent billers from manually filling the secondary before the data is available.
Step 1.2
Medicare / auto-forwarding payers: enable "submits to secondary payers" in insurer settings
Clinic Admin
Task
Toggle "Insurer submits claims to secondary payers" on the primary insurer settings (e.g. Medicare)
▶
Pain Point
When Medicare automatically forwards a claim to the patient's secondary insurer, Jane must be configured to detect this — via the "submits to secondary payers" toggle — or billers accidentally re-submit the secondary themselves, creating a duplicate claim
Community"If Medicare or other carriers automatically forward to secondary, EMR must detect this to avoid duplicate claims... They just want to make sure the 'Insurer submits claims to secondary payers' is checked ON. Jane will auto-mark the secondary claim as submitted." — Community and Alexa, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-detect forwarding behaviour from the ERA itself when a secondary response arrives — if Jane sees a secondary ERA linked to a primary submission that hasn't been explicitly submitted as secondary by the biller, flag it as likely auto-forwarded rather than requiring the insurer-level toggle to be pre-configured.
2
Receive Primary ERA & Pass Balance to Secondary
Step 2.1
Process primary ERA — set remaining balance to "Collect from Next Payer"
Biller
Task
Edit primary EOB — change "Adjust by" dropdown to "Collect $ From Next Payer" for the remaining balance
▶
Pain Point
The "Collect $ from Next Payer" option in the primary EOB is easy to miss — billers who select "Adjust By" instead write off the remaining balance rather than passing it to the secondary, so the secondary claim never receives a balance to collect against
Community"They'll want to add the secondary insurer, edit those primary EOBs to pass the balance to the Next Payer, then they'll be able to apply the secondary payment." — Karen, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
When a secondary policy exists on an appointment and the biller is editing the primary EOB, surface a prompt: "This patient has a secondary policy — set the remaining $X to Collect from Next Payer?" to make the most common correct action the default rather than requiring the biller to know the correct dropdown choice.
Jane System
Task
Auto-populate secondary claim with primary payer data — amounts billed, primary payment, remaining balance, COB details
▶
Opportunity
When the primary ERA is received and the "Collect from Next Payer" balance is set, Jane pre-fills the secondary claim 837 with the primary payer's adjudication details — this is a genuine strength and reduces manual entry for straightforward US COB scenarios.
Pain Point
The secondary claim EOB's "Paid Amount" column can show the primary's full allowed amount rather than the true secondary net payment when COB deductibles are involved — requiring the biller to manually calculate what the secondary actually paid
Community"Jane then automatically copied over the Deductible Field and Max Amount Field and created the PBC invoice owing as $2.15, when PBC actually paid $27.15. This resulted in a credit in PBC." — Christine K, #pt-insurance
3
Submit & Process Secondary Claim (US)
Step 3.1
Submit secondary claim electronically with primary payer info included
Biller
Task
Review pre-filled secondary claim and submit from the Unsubmitted folder
▶
Pain Point
Secondary COB denials frequently cite "CO-22: this care may be covered by another payer" or "MA04: secondary payment cannot be considered without identity or payment information from the primary payer" — usually caused by incomplete or incorrectly formatted primary adjudication data in the secondary 837
Community"CO 22, $250.00: This care may be covered by another payer per coordination of benefits. N36: Claim must meet primary payer's processing requirements before we can consider payment." — Real denial on a secondary claim, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Pre-flight validation on secondary claims — checking that the primary payer info, claim control number, and adjudication amounts are correctly populated before the 837 is transmitted — would catch the most common secondary COB denial causes before submission.
Biller
Task
Receive secondary ERA — record payment and confirm final patient balance
▶
Pain Point
After both insurers respond, calculating the true patient responsibility (what remains after both payers' adjustments, deductibles, and coinsurance) is a manual calculation that Jane does not surface automatically — billers must compare primary and secondary EOBs to arrive at the correct patient balance
Community"Accurate remaining balance calculation — after each payer response and/or manually input — appears to be missing." — Community feedback, #jane_community_posts_fb
Suggested Opportunity
A patient responsibility summary panel — showing primary paid, secondary paid, total adjustments, and the final patient balance, updated in real time as each ERA is processed — would replace the manual calculation that billers currently do across multiple EOB views.
4
Canadian COB — Significant Integration Gaps
Step 4.1
CA — Identify which primary/secondary combinations Jane can process electronically
Clinic Admin
Task
Check the CA COB compatibility matrix — most secondary combinations are NOT electronically submittable via Jane
▶
Pain Point
For Canadian clinics, TELUS eClaims does not support secondary claims or coordination of benefits at all — Jane can only submit primary claims through TELUS. Any secondary coverage must be handled directly by the insurer or by the patient submitting their own receipt
Community"Jane can only submit primary claims through TELUS eClaims. The adjudication details required for secondary processing can't be sent electronically via TELUS, which is why the submission fails." — Isa, #support_ca_insurance
Opportunity
The PBC PROVIDERnet integration is the notable exception — PBC auto-coordinates benefits when the claim is submitted via PROVIDERnet and the patient has a secondary policy. Jane auto-adds the secondary policy and handles the COB payment. Expanding this auto-coordination to more Canadian integrations is the clearest long-term opportunity.
Step 4.2
CA — PBC as primary: auto-coordination happens; PBC COB credit issues surface post-adjudication
Jane System
Task
PBC auto-coordinates with secondary plan during adjudication — Jane auto-adds the secondary policy to the patient profile
▶
Opportunity
PBC auto-coordination is the most advanced COB experience in Jane's Canadian integrations — PBC handles the secondary adjudication automatically and Jane reflects both payments. The only step required from the clinic is submitting to PBC as primary via PROVIDERnet.
Biller
Task
Reconcile PBC COB payment amounts — deductible fields are sometimes miscalculated, creating unintended credits
▶
Pain Point
When PBC coordinates benefits with a secondary plan, Jane can miscalculate the PBC invoice amount — copying the COB deductible field incorrectly and creating a credit on the PBC insurer account instead of applying the full secondary payment to the claim
Community"Jane then automatically copied over $32.76 into the Deductible Field and $61.05 into the Max Amount Field and created the PBC invoice owing as $28.29, when PBC actually paid $61.05. This resulted in a $61.05 PBC payment only partially applied, leaving a credit in PBC." — Christine K, #pt-insurance
Suggested Opportunity
A reconciliation check after PBC COB processing — confirming that the sum of the primary payment + secondary payment equals the total submitted amount before any credits appear — would catch this miscalculation automatically rather than requiring the biller to discover it manually later.
Step 4.3
CA — PBC R&C limits prevent secondary forwarding; patient must submit own receipt
Biller
Task
Explain to patient why PBC didn't pass the balance to their secondary — R&C limit reached
▶
Pain Point
PBC applies Reasonable & Customary (R&C) limits — when the limit is reached, the remaining balance is classified as non-eligible and not forwarded to the secondary plan at all. Patients and clinic staff are frequently confused and assume this is a Jane error rather than PBC policy
Community"PBC paid up to the maximum allowed under R&C on the primary plan. Any remaining amount is treated as patient responsibility. Because that balance isn't eligible, there's nothing for the secondary policy to coordinate." — Crystal J., #support_ca_insurance
Suggested Opportunity
When a PBC COB claim returns with no secondary forwarding, surface a plain-language explanation in Jane — "PBC did not forward the remaining balance to your secondary plan (likely due to Reasonable & Customary limits). The patient may need to submit their receipt directly." — to avoid confusion and unnecessary support escalations.
