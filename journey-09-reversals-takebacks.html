Reversals & Takebacks

How a biller handles it when an insurer reclaims a prior payment — identifying the type of reversal, locating the original payment across multiple ERAs, unapplying it to create credits, tracking where money needs to move, and reconciling the final state. Covers all five reversal scenarios: neutral, positive, negative same-ERA, negative cross-ERA (takeback), and paper/letter-only takebacks. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #pt-insurance, #pt-billing-service, #hot_seat_support, and #ask_support_anything.

G1 Biller can identify what type of reversal they're dealing with without escalating to a billing specialist
G2 Biller can find the original overpaid claim and its payment without opening Jane in 3 tabs
G3 The credit created by unapplying a payment flows automatically to the claim that needs it

Discover a Reversal in an ERA
Step 0.1
Biller spots reversal entries while processing an ERA
Biller
Task
Identify "Reversal of Previous Payment" entries in the ERA response list
▶
Pain Point
Reversals are rare but high-pain — when they appear, billers must locate old payments, track claims across multiple ERAs, and remember where money was taken from and where it needs to go. They create mental "IOUs" and require Jane open in 3 tabs to see the full picture
Community"Reversals are rare but high-pain. Users need ways to actually complete their workflow of reconciling reversals without needing to have Jane open in 3 tabs to see what's happening." — Mosh, #pt-insurance
Suggested Opportunity
A "reversal detected" callout at the top of an ERA — summarizing how many reversals are present and linking directly to the affected original payments — would give billers a starting point before they dive into individual response entries.
Biller
Task
Determine: is this a neutral reversal, positive reversal, or negative reversal (takeback)?
▶
Pain Point
All reversal types look nearly identical in Jane's ERA list — a neutral reversal (insurer reprocessed but paid the same), a positive reversal (insurer is paying more), and a negative takeback (insurer is clawing back money) all appear as "Reversal of Previous Payment" entries with no clear type label
Community"Reversal overpayment takebacks still scare me." — Crystal J., #support, expressing how intimidating these are even for experienced support staff
Opportunity
Label reversal entries by type at a glance — "Neutral Reversal — no action needed," "Positive Reversal — insurer is paying more," "Negative Reversal / Takeback — payment is being reclaimed" — with a one-sentence action summary for each. This would eliminate the most common reversal support escalation pattern.
1
Path A: Neutral Reversal (No Action Needed)
Step 1.1
Insurer reprocessed the claim but paid the same amount — ERA shows $0 payment
Biller
Task
Confirm the reversal response matches the original EOB amount, then mark as Reviewed
▶
Pain Point
The neutral reversal (insurer reprocessed a claim identically) shows a $0 payment in the ERA — which looks like a problem. Billers frequently assume something went wrong and escalate, when the correct action is simply to mark it as reviewed with no EOB changes
Community"This is a neutral reversal. The insurer reprocessed the claim, but didn't make a change in how they processed it. That's why on this ERA, the payment amount is $0.00. They can go ahead and mark the reversal response as reviewed." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-detect neutral reversals (where the reversal amount exactly matches the corresponding new response) and pre-label them in the ERA as "No action needed — mark reviewed" to eliminate the anxiety and support calls they generate.
2
Path B: Positive Reversal (Insurer Paying More)
Step 2.1
Insurer reverses original payment and reprocesses to a higher amount
Biller
Task
Disconnect the original payment from the claim, re-record the original amount as outstanding, then apply the new higher amount
▶
Pain Point
The multi-step positive reversal workflow is non-intuitive — billers must: (1) disconnect the original full payment, (2) re-record the original paid amount with "Leave Outstanding" instead of "Adjust By," (3) go back to the new ERA, and (4) record the updated amount. Missing any step creates a credit or balance discrepancy
Community"Instruct the clinic to keep the Billed Amount fields to be the original value of the CPT code, and change the Paid Amount section's drop down from 'Adjust by' to 'Leave Outstanding,' then hit Apply Changes. Now they can go back to the new remittance, hit View for the updated payments and edit each CPT code to match the new paid amount totals." — Earl, #support_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A guided "process positive reversal" flow — triggered when Jane detects a reversal + corresponding higher payment on the same ERA — would walk billers through the 4-step process without requiring them to hold the context across multiple parts of Jane.
3
Path C: Negative Reversal / Takeback — Same ERA
Step 3.1
Insurer reverses a prior payment AND takes the money back on the same ERA
Biller
Task
Change the original claim's paid amounts to $0 to create insurer credits, then apply those credits to reconcile the ERA
▶
Opportunity
Same-ERA takebacks are the more tractable scenario — because the overpaid claims are explicitly referenced in the same ERA, the old payment and new payment are both visible. Jane could auto-suggest the credit application step by identifying the reversed claim and the net-zero provider adjustment on the same ERA.
Community"For the two negative reversals, they'll want to change the paid amounts to $0.00. This creates an insurer credit of $80.19 + $76.49. This credit should be used to finish reconciling the claims on this current ERA." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Biller
Task
Locate the insurer credit created by the reversal and apply it to the correct claim on the ERA
▶
Pain Point
Once a reversal creates an insurer credit, the biller must know to navigate to Insurer Invoices → Insurer Name → Available Credit to find the newly created credit and apply it. There is no in-ERA prompt pointing them there — billers who don't know this workflow get stuck with unreconciled credits
Community"Head to Insurer Invoices › Insurer Name › Available Credit, locate the payment with the credit that was created from the reversal, click View — there is the Available to Apply amount of $56.66." — Katie, walking a support driver through the 7-step process, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Surface the newly created insurer credit directly in the ERA view after a reversal is processed — "A credit of $X was created. Apply it to a claim on this ERA?" — rather than requiring the biller to navigate to a separate Insurer Invoices section to find it.
4
Path D: Cross-ERA Takeback (The Hardest Path)
Step 4.1
Insurer communicates the overpayment on one ERA, then takes the money on a future ERA
Biller
Task
Receive future ERA with net negative provider-level adjustment — no claim-level reference to what was overpaid
▶
Pain Point
In the cross-ERA takeback scenario, the ERA that takes the money contains no reference to the original overpaid claims — that information only appears in a letter the insurer sends separately outside of Jane. Billers must reference that letter to know which original claims to hunt down
Community"The insurer never communicates via the 835 what previous claims the money is being recovered from. That info is only in the letters insurers send, and those aren't communicated via clearinghouses." — John Whaley, #pt-insurance
Opportunity
If Jane had a way to ingest the insurer's overpayment letter (via file upload), AI could parse it for the claim identifiers referenced and automatically surface the original claims the biller needs to unapply — a possibility John Whaley explicitly flagged as "more solvable with AI tools now."
Step 4.2
Biller hunts for the original overpaid claims across historical ERAs
Biller
Task
Use the claim identifiers from the insurer letter to locate the original payment in Jane
▶
Pain Point
Tracking down the original overpaid claim requires navigating Claim Submissions → Search, cross-referencing with the letter, finding the correct remittance, and opening the payment — all in separate parts of Jane. There is no "find this original claim" shortcut starting from the takeback ERA
Community"You need to ask which previous claim and which remittance the insurer is taking back money from — this will have been communicated to the clinic at some point (and you won't have access to that info) so they need to provide it to you." — Ly, #hot_seat_support
Pain Point
Tracking down multiple ERA response links for a single claim is also impossible in a new tab — right-clicking "View Response" in the ERA list doesn't open in a new tab, making multi-claim cross-ERA investigation extremely cumbersome
Community"When looking at the ERA list and right-clicking View Response you aren't able to open these into another tab. Compare this to any other clickable link in Jane that does pop out into another tab — it makes for a poor investigative experience for both Drivers and Billers." — Earl, #ask_support_anything
Suggested Opportunity
Making all ERA response "View" links openable in a new tab would be the single smallest fix with the largest impact on investigative workflows — applicable to both reversals and any multi-ERA claim tracking scenario.
Step 4.3
Biller unapplies original payment, creates credit, applies to takeback ERA
Biller
Task
Unapply the original insurer payment to create a credit, navigate to the takeback ERA, and apply that credit
▶
Pain Point
The full cross-ERA takeback reconciliation requires: (1) find the original ERA, (2) unapply its payment, (3) navigate to the takeback ERA, (4) find the newly created credit in Insurer Invoices, (5) apply the credit to the right claim. All five steps are disconnected in Jane with no guided flow
Community"You need to find the remittance that had the reversal occur, head to the Payments section, hit View for the reversed remittance, manually record the new payment, then head back to the later ERA and manually mark the claim as reviewed." — Earl, #chat_hot_seat
Opportunity
A "reconcile takeback" guided workflow — starting from the takeback ERA, linking to the original claim via a search or letter upload, and walking through the unapply → credit → apply steps in sequence — would reduce a 5-step multi-tab process to a single in-ERA flow.
5
Path E: Paper / Letter-Only Takeback
Step 5.1
Clinic receives a takeback demand by mail — no ERA, no electronic record in Jane
Biller
Task
Manually process the takeback using only a paper letter — no ERA arrives in Jane to trigger the workflow
▶
Pain Point
Some insurers (especially for out-of-network clinics or paper check payers) send overpayment demand letters by snail mail with no corresponding electronic ERA — the biller must process the entire takeback manually in Jane with no automated trigger, no record linking, and no tracking mechanism
Community"AO received an insurance reversal (negative takeback) by mail. They had mistakenly billed Humana when Humana wanted them to bill their alternative medicine partner Wholehealthliving. They received a takeback request via snail mail because they do not have ERA/EOB with them directly." — Support transfer, #support_usa
Suggested Opportunity
A "record manual takeback" entry point in Jane — where a biller can upload the letter, link it to the original claim, and process the credit/reconciliation flow identically to how an ERA takeback is handled — would eliminate the need to handle paper takebacks entirely outside the system.
Biller
Task
Track the outstanding IOU — the insurer will recoup from a future ERA without warning
▶
Pain Point
After acknowledging a takeback, the biller must remember to watch for the future ERA where the insurer actually deducts the amount — sometimes weeks or months later. There is no in-Jane mechanism to flag an "outstanding takeback IOU" or alert the biller when the matching deduction ERA arrives
Community"It is a good idea for them to keep track of these types of overpayments for future reference." — Jordan, #hot_seat_support — but this tracking happens entirely outside Jane
Opportunity
An "outstanding takeback" tracker in Jane — where billers can log a pending takeback with the insurer, amount, and relevant claims — and be notified when a future ERA from that insurer shows a matching provider-level deduction, would replace the current entirely manual external tracking system.
