Insurer Reporting & Analytics

How a biller or clinic owner uses Jane's reporting tools to understand insurance AR, track outstanding claims, identify aging balances, and measure insurer performance. Covers the AR Report, Claim Submissions Search, the Insurer Invoices view, and the significant gap between what Jane provides and what high-volume insurance practices need. Signal from #support_reports, #support_usa_billing, #pt-insurance, #jane_community_posts_fb, and #community.

G1 Biller can identify every outstanding insurer claim and exactly why it's outstanding in a single report
G2 AR totals are trustworthy — no ghost balances, no COB double-counting, no unexplained discrepancies
G3 Clinic owner can see insurer performance (denial rate, days-to-pay, collection rate) without leaving Jane

Check the AR Report
Step 0.1
Biller opens AR Report — views outstanding balances by insurer and aging bucket
Biller
Task
Filter AR Report by insurer and review aging buckets (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90–120, 120+)
▶
Pain Point
The AR Report is aggregated — it shows totals per insurer and aging bucket, but clicking through to see which specific patients and invoices make up a $500 120+ bucket requires navigating to the insurer page with different filters. There's no single drill-through that shows "these 7 invoices total $500"
Community"She's wanting to see a detailed breakdown of which specific claims and patients within a particular insurer are contributing to claims outstanding." — Stine, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
A drill-down view within the AR Report (currently SU-only via an internal extension) was shipped after a 2025 hackathon and considered for broader release. Making this available to clinics — showing individual invoices contributing to each AR bucket — is the clearest near-term improvement to the report's actionability.
Biller
Task
Reconcile AR Report totals against insurer page — discrepancies are common and time-consuming to diagnose
▶
Pain Point
The AR Report and the Insurer page filter work differently — the AR Report is an "as-of" snapshot based on appointment dates, while the insurer page filters by date range as of today. Balances that match in one view don't match in the other, causing billers to spend significant time reconciling numbers that should be identical
Community"If we filter the report for Insurance Accounts on June 26th, WSBC = $536.00. When we clicked the corresponding links to hop into the Insurer Page, we could see there were no outstanding for WSBC." — Kelli, #support_reports
Suggested Opportunity
A single consistent "what is outstanding right now" view that links the AR Report bucket directly to the insurer page with the same date filter pre-applied — so that clicking a $536 WSBC bucket in the AR Report opens the insurer page pre-filtered to show exactly those invoices.
Step 0.2
Biller investigates ghost balances — amounts on the AR Report with no matching invoice
Biller
Task
Track down amounts appearing as outstanding on the AR Report that cannot be found anywhere in the insurer profile
▶
Pain Point
Ghost AR balances — amounts showing as outstanding on the AR Report with no matching invoice in the insurer profile — are a recurring and time-consuming problem. They're caused by stale deleted invoices, voided claims that didn't clean up correctly, unapplied payments, and COB double-counting. Diagnosing them requires Support escalation
Community"There are two outstanding amounts of $180 showing for more than 120 days on the AR Report, but we can't find the amounts in the insurer profiles and aren't sure where those amounts are being pulled from." — Kaitlyn, #support_billing
Pain Point
COB (dual-policy) appointments inflate the AR Report — when both primary and secondary invoices are outstanding simultaneously, the same appointment's value appears twice in the AR total. High-volume dual-payer clinics report their AR as "inaccurate" and "unusable" because of this double-counting
Community"Jane is inflating the totals on their AR Report with those duplicate outstanding amounts — once under the Primary Insurer and again under the Secondary Insurer." — Catherine, #support_us_medical_glowup
Opportunity
A COB-aware AR calculation that shows the net expected collection per appointment (not the sum of all open invoices) would eliminate the most common category of AR inflation complaints from dual-payer practices.
Biller
Task
Investigate AR Report expanded invoice list — discovers it shows all staff, not the filtered staff member
▶
Pain Point
When filtering the AR Report by staff member and then expanding an insurer to see contributing invoices, the expanded list shows all invoices for that insurer across the entire clinic — ignoring the staff filter. This is a known limitation (CFP-2382) that causes billers to believe their AR is contaminated with other practitioners' data
Community"The Contributing Invoices list that expands when you click the arrow under an insurer doesn't actually filter by staff member — it shows every invoice tied to that insurer across all staff, regardless of who you've filtered to." — Abbey, #support_reports

Track Outstanding Claims & Follow Up
Step 1.1
Biller uses Claim Submissions Search to find actionable outstanding claims
Biller
Task
Go to Billing → Claim Submissions → Search, filter by All Outstanding, export to CSV for follow-up
▶
Opportunity
The Claim Submissions Search export is the most actionable outstanding-claims report Jane offers — patient name, DOB, insurer, subscriber number in one export. Billers who discover this use it as their primary AR follow-up tool. It's a genuine strength, though it requires knowing to look here rather than the AR Report.
Pain Point
Claim Submissions Search and the AR Report are completely separate tools with no cross-linking — a biller who sees a $2,000 BCBS bucket in the AR Report has no one-click path to see the specific outstanding BCBS claims in Claim Submissions Search with the same date range pre-applied
Community"The AR report is great for honing in on a specific insurer, but the Claims Submissions Search area gives a bit more information. Exporting gives a lot more flexibility for sorting and filtering." — Katie Simmons, #support_usa_billing
Biller
Task
Set monthly cadence to review claims older than 30 days — no automated aging alert exists in Jane
▶
Pain Point
There is no proactive alert or automated workflow in Jane for aging claims — billers must manually remember to run the Claim Submissions Search report on a schedule, or claims quietly age past 90/120 days without anyone noticing until the AR Report shows a large 120+ bucket
Community"Setting up a monthly routine to search for claims older than 30 days is a great way to catch issues." — Katie Simmons, recommending a manual workaround for a gap Jane doesn't fill, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
An automated aging alert — "You have 12 claims older than 30 days with no response — here's the list" delivered weekly to the biller — would replace the current entirely manual follow-up cadence recommendation.
Step 1.2
Biller supplements Jane with ClaimMD reporting for data Jane doesn't surface
Biller
Task
Log into ClaimMD portal to access clearinghouse-level reporting Jane doesn't provide
▶
Pain Point
Insurance reporting is acknowledged internally as "one of our biggest insurance weaknesses" — the go-to recommendation for clinics who need more than Jane's AR Report provides is to look at ClaimMD's own reporting, because Jane doesn't report on denial rates, days-to-pay, or clearinghouse-level claim status trends
Community"As far as our insurance reporting goes, this is one of our biggest insurance weaknesses. One of our go-tos is to suggest looking at Claim.MD's reporting, as they fill some gaps for Jane." — John Whaley, #jane_community_posts_fb
Pain Point
High-volume insurance practices have resorted to third-party tools — Trust Driven Care, custom scripts, external spreadsheets — because Jane's reporting can't support the AR aging, denial rate, and collection rate analysis they need to run their businesses
Community"Several folks shared workarounds — custom scripts, ClaimMD reports, third-party tools — while others name-dropped Trust Driven Care as the analytics band-aid they've resorted to." — Community digest, #community
2
Review Insurer Performance
Step 2.1
Clinic owner tries to understand how well each insurer is paying — denial rate, days-to-pay, collection rate
Clinic Owner
Task
Attempt to find denial rate, average days-to-pay, and collection rate per insurer in Jane
▶
Pain Point
None of these metrics exist in Jane — denial rate, days-to-pay by insurer, collection rate, or clean claim rate are completely absent. A clinic owner wanting to decide whether to continue accepting a particular payer has no data in Jane to support that decision
Community"Clinics are hungry for real BI, dashboards, and reliable AR to run their businesses — the data is here, the workflow and programming isn't." — Community feedback, #jane_community_posts_fb
Suggested Opportunity
An insurer performance dashboard — showing denial rate, average days-to-first-payment, collection rate (paid / billed), and 120+ aging amount per insurer — would give clinic owners the business intelligence to manage their payer mix, currently available nowhere in Jane.
Clinic Owner
Task
Export AR Report and Claim Submissions data to a spreadsheet, manually build performance metrics
▶
Pain Point
The CSV export from Claim Submissions Search is the closest thing to a raw data source — but building denial rates or days-to-pay from it requires manual pivot tables. Clinics that manage this at all are doing it in Excel or Google Sheets, outside of Jane entirely
Community"Accurate AR data, please, plus the ability to export everything to CSV so clinics can run real aging reports instead of relying on 'hope'." — Michael, via community digest, #community
Opportunity
A full CSV export from the AR Report itself — including invoice-level detail, aging bucket, insurer, patient, and claim status — would let data-savvy billers build their own analytics in external tools until Jane builds native dashboards. Currently only the Claim Submissions Search offers this kind of exportable detail.
Step 2.2
Biller reviews Partially Paid and Claims Outstanding views for follow-up queues
Biller
Task
Check Billing → Claim Submissions → Partially Paid report to find claims where the insurer paid less than billed
▶
Pain Point
The Partially Paid report frequently surfaces invoices as partially paid even when the full amount has been collected — usually because a group invoice is fully paid but the individual insurance invoice shows a balance. Billers waste time investigating "partially paid" claims that aren't actually outstanding
Community"The partially paid report is showing 4 invoices as no charge — if you go into the invoice it shows as a balance owing but if you follow the payments you can see it is already paid." — Taneeya, #support_reports
Suggested Opportunity
When a group invoice has been fully collected but individual component invoices still show a balance, exclude those from the Partially Paid report — or surface a clear "payment received via group invoice" label so billers know no action is needed.

The Reporting Gap — What High-Volume Practices Need but Jane Doesn't Provide
Step 3.1
Summary of known missing reporting capabilities for insurance-heavy practices
Clinic Owner
Task
Understand denial rate by insurer — what % of submitted claims are denied by each payer
▶
Pain Point
No denial rate reporting exists in Jane. Tracking denial frequency by insurer, by CPT code, or by denial reason requires manually counting denial ERA responses — a task that takes hours and can't be scheduled or automated.
Suggested Opportunity
Denial rate by insurer (% claims denied, top denial reason codes) as a built-in report would let clinics identify which payers and code combinations need attention — and whether a payer's denial pattern is worth engaging about.
Clinic Owner
Task
Understand days-to-payment by insurer — how long each payer takes from submission to ERA
▶
Pain Point
Average days-to-payment per insurer — the most basic RCM metric — is not available anywhere in Jane. Clinics cannot tell from Jane data whether Insurer A consistently takes 12 days to pay and Insurer B takes 45 days, making cash flow planning impossible within the product.
Suggested Opportunity
Days-to-payment (submission date to ERA date) calculated per insurer using existing Jane data — no external data needed — would be a high-value, low-complexity addition to the insurer profile page or a dedicated insurance analytics section.
Clinic Owner
Task
Understand clean claim rate — what % of claims are paid without requiring a resubmission or correction
▶
Pain Point
Clean claim rate — the industry-standard measure of billing quality — is not tracked in Jane at all. Clinics that want to know whether their billing setup is generating unnecessary rejections have no way to measure this without manually auditing their claim history.
Suggested Opportunity
Clean claim rate (claims paid on first submission / total claims) per insurer and per billing code would give clinic owners a direct measure of billing quality — and would motivate investments in pre-submission validation like Roux rules.
