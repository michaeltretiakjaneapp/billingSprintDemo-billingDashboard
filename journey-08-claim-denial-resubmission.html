Claim Denial & Resubmission

Jane · Insurance Billing · Journey #8 — How billers identify, investigate, correct, and resubmit denied claims, and what happens when corrections fail.

G1 Understand the denial reason quickly and accurately
G2 Correct and resubmit before timely filing deadlines
G3 Recover full payment owed for services rendered
G4 Minimize write-offs and preventable AR aging
G5 Identify denial patterns to prevent future rejections
0
Denial Received
Step 0.1
ERA / EOB Arrives With Denial
Biller
Task
Scans ERA batch for denied claims
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Denied claims arrive mixed into routine ERA payment batches with no visual separation — billers must scan every line item to find them, creating a high-cognitive-load triage step every time an ERA is processed.
Suggested Pain Point
No denial notification or alert exists — billers may not notice a denial until an AR aging report surfaces the unpaid claim days or weeks later, pushing the correction dangerously close to timely filing deadlines.
Suggested Opportunity
A dedicated denial-flagged queue that automatically separates denied line items from processed payments in the ERA view, surfacing them as an action item requiring attention.
Jane System
Task
Marks claim as denied and updates status
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Denied claims do not receive a visually distinct status in the claims list — they appear alongside submitted and paid claims, making it impossible to filter the view to "only show denied" without opening every ERA individually.
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-set a distinct "Denied" status badge on claims when an ERA denial code is received, visible from the Unsubmitted and claim list views without requiring the biller to open each ERA.
Step 0.2
Triage the Denial
Biller
Task
Identifies denial category from CARC / RARC codes
▶
Suggested Pain Point
CARC (Claim Adjustment Reason Codes) and RARC (Remittance Advice Remark Codes) are highly technical — billers must navigate away from Jane to external lookup tools like Find-A-Code or the WEDI CARC list to translate codes like CO-4 or CO-97 into actionable language.
Suggested Pain Point
Multiple denial codes can appear on a single claim (e.g. a primary CARC plus a secondary RARC) with no guidance on which to address first or whether they are related.
Suggested Opportunity
Inline CARC/RARC plain-language translations displayed directly on the denied claim in Jane — "CO-4: The service billed is inconsistent with the modifier used" — so billers never need to leave the product to understand a denial.
Biller
Task
Checks timely filing deadline for this payer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Each payer has different timely filing limits — some are 90 days from date of service, others are 6 months or 1 year. There is no timely filing countdown or deadline indicator in Jane. Billers must track this manually or in a spreadsheet, and a missed deadline means permanent write-off.
Suggested Opportunity
A timely filing deadline indicator on each denied claim, calculated from the payer's known filing limit and the date of service, with a visual urgency flag (e.g. yellow = 30 days left, red = 7 days left).
1
Investigate the Denial
Step 1.1
Decode the Denial Reason
Biller
Task
Researches root cause using external tools and payer portals
▶
Suggested Pain Point
No in-product denial reason library exists — billers navigate away from Jane to external coding sites, payer portals, or Google to understand why a claim was denied and what the correct fix is.
Suggested Pain Point
The same denial reason code can mean different things at different payers (e.g. CO-97 at Aetna vs CO-97 at BCBS). Without payer-specific context, billers risk applying the wrong fix.
Biller
Task
Calls insurer provider services if denial reason is ambiguous
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Provider services hold times of 30–90 minutes are common for major payers. A single ambiguous denial can consume a large portion of a biller's day — and smaller clinics with one biller face the worst of this.
Suggested Pain Point
Call outcomes (reference numbers, rep names, notes, promised resolution dates) must be stored outside Jane in spreadsheets or sticky notes. There is no way to log a phone inquiry against a specific claim.
Suggested Opportunity
A call log field on the claim where billers can record the date, rep name, reference number, and outcome of insurer phone calls — keeping the full paper trail attached to the claim.
Step 1.2
Gather Missing Information
Biller
Task
Flags claim for practitioner correction and context
▶
Suggested Pain Point
No native workflow in Jane allows a biller to notify a practitioner that their chart needs a correction before the claim can be resubmitted. Billers resort to Slack messages, emails, or verbal nudges — creating an informal, untrackable handoff.
Suggested Opportunity
A "Request Correction" action on the denied claim that creates a task in the practitioner's Jane to-do list, including the denial reason and what needs to be fixed — creating a traceable, in-product handoff.
Practitioner
Task
Reviews denial context and corrects the chart if needed
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Practitioners have zero visibility into claim status or denial reasons in Jane. They cannot see if billing from one of their appointments has been denied or is awaiting a correction without a separate message from the biller — billing is entirely siloed from clinical workflows.
Suggested Opportunity
A billing status indicator on the appointment card in the schedule view — "Claim Denied — Correction Needed" — giving practitioners ambient awareness of billing issues tied to their appointments without requiring a separate communication channel.
2
Correct the Claim
Step 2.1
Make Corrections to Claim Data
Biller
Task
Edits billing codes, diagnosis codes, or claim fields
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Editing a submitted claim silently bounces it back to Unsubmitted with no confirmation or warning (surfaced in Journey #6). Billers may not realize the claim needs to be re-batched and resubmitted — leading to it sitting in Unsubmitted indefinitely while the timely filing deadline passes.
Suggested Pain Point
If the denial was caused by the wrong insurer being selected, the insurer field is locked after submission. The only fix is to archive the invoice and recreate it from scratch — destroying the original submission history and breaking the claim's paper trail. (Active project: #proj-changeinsurer)
Suggested Opportunity
When a claim is edited and bounced back to Unsubmitted, show a prominent "Correction Saved — Ready to Resubmit" confirmation so billers know the next step explicitly rather than discovering it through the AR aging report.
Biller
Task
Attaches supporting documentation required by insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Clinical documentation required for medical necessity denials — operative reports, progress notes, prior authorization letters — cannot be attached to a claim in Jane. Billers must submit documentation through a separate payer portal, fax, or mail, breaking the workflow and creating a parallel paper trail.
Suggested Opportunity
A document attachment capability on the claim for prior auth letters, medical records, and appeal cover letters — keeping all claim-related materials in one place and enabling a future "submit with documentation" electronic pathway.
Step 2.2
Verify Before Resubmission
Biller
Task
Confirms corrections are complete and accurate
▶
Suggested Pain Point
No comparison view exists between the original submitted claim and the corrected version. Billers work from memory to ensure they haven't missed a field — an error in the correction leads to a second denial and another timely filing window lost.
Suggested Opportunity
A "diff view" before resubmission that highlights exactly what changed between the original claim and the corrected version — giving billers a clear checklist before they commit to resubmission.
Jane System
Task
Carries forward original claim ICN / TCN reference
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The payer's original claim control number (ICN or TCN) from the ERA is required on the corrected claim's resubmission fields but is not surfaced in Jane after ERA processing. Billers must look it up in the clearinghouse portal separately — an extra manual step that introduces transcription error risk.
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-populate the original payer ICN/TCN from the ERA into the corrected claim's resubmission reference field, eliminating the manual lookup and reducing the risk of a second denial caused by a missing or incorrect reference number.
3
Resubmit the Claim
Step 3.1
Configure Resubmission Fields
Biller
Task
Selects correct CLM05-3 resubmission code
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The distinction between Resubmission Code 6 (Corrected Claim) and Code 7 (Replacement of Prior Claim) is confusing and payer-specific. Selecting the wrong one triggers a secondary denial. No in-product guidance explains when to use each code. (Code 6 deprecated at some clearinghouses — surfaced in Journey #6.)
Suggested Pain Point
Resubmission Code 8 (Void/Cancel of Prior Claim) is selectable with no warning or confirmation prompt. Accidentally submitting a void reversal when a correction was intended is extremely difficult to undo and can trigger a recovery demand from the payer.
Suggested Opportunity
Contextual resubmission code guidance based on the denial reason: "This claim was denied with CO-4 (modifier issue) — use Code 6: Corrected Claim" — reducing the cognitive burden on billers and preventing secondary denials caused by incorrect code selection.
Step 3.2
Submit the Corrected Claim
Biller
Task
Marks corrected claim ready and batches for submission
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Corrected claims near timely filing deadlines are queued in the same batch as routine new claims with no priority mechanism. An urgent resubmission due in 3 days competes equally with a fresh claim that has 6 months of runway.
Suggested Opportunity
A priority flag on corrected claims within 30 days of their timely filing deadline, surfacing them at the top of the submission queue and alerting the biller if a submission batch is missed.
Jane System
Task
Generates corrected 837 EDI with resubmission fields
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The corrected 837 must include REF*F8 (original reference number loop) and the correct CLM05-3 resubmission code in the EDI. If either is missing or wrong, the clearinghouse or payer rejects the corrected claim as a duplicate — compounding the problem with another denial.
Suggested Opportunity
Validate that the resubmission code and original reference number are present and syntactically correct before generating the 837 — surfacing any gaps as a pre-submission warning rather than discovering them via a clearinghouse rejection days later.
Step 3.3
Monitor Resubmission Status
Biller
Task
Tracks corrected claim through to acknowledgement
▶
Suggested Pain Point
After resubmission, the corrected claim and the original denied claim appear as separate unlinked records in Jane. The full paper trail — original submission, denial, correction, resubmission, payment — is fragmented across multiple claim records with no native way to see them as a single lifecycle.
Suggested Opportunity
A claim history thread view that links all related claim records for a single visit into a sequential timeline: original submission → ERA denial → corrected claim → resubmission → payment response — making the full story visible in one place.
Clearinghouse
Task
Processes corrected 837 and routes to payer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
If the clearinghouse flags the corrected claim as a duplicate (e.g. because the original ICN was missing or the resubmission code was wrong), the rejection message is generic — "Duplicate Claim" — with no indication of which original claim it's conflicting with or what the biller should change.
4
Resolution & Follow-Up
Step 4.1
Evaluate Response to Corrected Claim
Biller
Task
Reviews ERA response to corrected claim
▶
Suggested Pain Point
If the corrected claim is also denied, there is no structured next-step guidance in Jane. Billers must independently decide whether to appeal, write off, bill the secondary insurer, or escalate — with no decision framework and no tracking of which path was chosen.
Suggested Opportunity
A denial resolution decision tree on second denial: "Corrected claim denied again — next steps: File Formal Appeal / Write Off / Bill Secondary Insurer / Escalate to Payer Relations" — with the chosen path recorded against the claim for audit trail purposes.
Biller
Task
Files formal appeal if payment is refused after correction
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Formal appeals require assembling a cover letter, clinical documentation, and a corrected claim together in a single submission packet. Jane has no appeal assembly, tracking, or submission capability — billers must manage the entire appeal workflow outside the product.
Suggested Opportunity
An appeal packet builder that pulls the claim data, linked clinical notes from the chart, and a templated appeal letter into a single exportable PDF — reducing assembly time and ensuring no required component is missing.
Step 4.2
Apply Adjustments or Write-Off
Biller
Task
Applies contractual adjustment or routes balance to patient
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Distinguishing between a CO (Contractual Obligation — do not bill patient) and a PR (Patient Responsibility — bill patient) denial type is critical but requires manual interpretation of the ERA's adjustment reason codes. Misclassifying a CO as a PR means incorrectly billing a patient; missing a PR means the clinic absorbs avoidable revenue loss.
Suggested Opportunity
Auto-apply CO contractual adjustments directly from ERA data and auto-create a patient invoice for PR balances — surfacing the right next action without requiring the biller to manually interpret adjustment group codes from the raw EOB.
Step 4.3
Denial Trend Analysis
Biller
Task
Reviews denial patterns to identify systemic issues
▶
Suggested Pain Point
No denial analytics exist in Jane. Which payers have the highest denial rates? Which CPT/ICD-10 combinations are most frequently rejected? Which practitioners generate the most denials? All of this analysis requires exporting data and building spreadsheets manually — turning a preventive intelligence task into a multi-hour ad-hoc project.
Suggested Opportunity
A denial trend dashboard in the Billing tab showing denial rate by payer, top denial codes over time, and denial rate by practitioner — enabling proactive prevention (fixing documentation habits, challenging payer rules) rather than purely reactive correction.
Jane System
Task
Surfaces denial pattern insights and prevention recommendations
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Proactive denial prevention recommendations surfaced at claim creation time: "This CPT/modifier combination has been denied by Aetna 4 times in the last 3 months — consider reviewing before submission." Turning historical denial data into forward-looking guardrails.
