Patient Insurance Policy Setup

How a patient's insurance coverage gets into Jane — via intake form self-service, staff manual entry, or front-desk assisted — through to staff review, approval, and attachment to an appointment. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #support_clinical_forms, #pt-insurance-foundations, #beta_insurance_usa, and #jane-ai-sdk.

G1 Patient can accurately enter their own insurance info during intake without needing to call the clinic
G2 Staff can quickly review and approve submitted policies without hunting for missing info
G3 The approved policy is correctly attached to the right appointment before billing
0
Patient Receives Intake Form
Step 0.1
Clinic sends intake form with insurance section enabled
Clinic Admin
Task
Configure intake form: enable insurance section, set required fields, set card image capture
▶
Pain Point
If the insurance section isn't set to required, patients commonly select "none" and skip it entirely — the clinic discovers missing policy info only when they try to bill
Community"The patients most likely selected 'none', which wouldn't require them to provide their images or enter any insurance info." — Sayyada, #support_charts
Suggested Opportunity
A warning at appointment booking if the patient has no insurance policy on file — and the clinic bills insurance — would catch missing policy info before the appointment happens.
Jane System
Task
Send intake form link to patient via email
▶
Suggested Opportunity
For returning patients, pre-fill the insurance section with their existing policy details — letting them confirm or update rather than re-entering everything from scratch.
1
Patient Fills In Insurance Info
Step 1.1
Patient sees insurance section & selects their insurer
Patient
Task
Browse insurer dropdown to find their insurance plan
▶
Pain Point
The insurer dropdown only shows what the clinic has pre-configured — patients whose insurer isn't listed hit a dead end with no way to enter a custom value or proceed
Community"The insurer dropdown only shows pre-configured insurers. Patients whose insurer isn't listed have no way to enter it, creating a dead end in the intake form." — Stine, #support_us_manualtherapy
Pain Point
Insurer names in Jane are set by the clinic — they often don't match the name on the patient's physical insurance card, causing patients to select the wrong insurer or give up
Community"We know that the names of insurers in Jane don't match patient cards… it'd be better to sunset the old table sooner rather than later since it'll only get more confusing." — John Whaley, #proj-insurance-upload-improvements
Opportunity
A native "Other / Not listed" free-text option in the insurer dropdown would eliminate the dead end for patients whose insurer isn't configured. Active Canny FR.
Step 1.2
Patient enters Member ID, group number & policy details
Patient
Task
Find and type in their Member/Subscriber ID from their insurance card
▶
Pain Point
Field labels like "Member ID" don't always match what's on the patient's card — every insurer names the number differently (Subscriber ID, Insured's ID, Claim #), causing confusion and wrong entries
Community"The 'Member ID' is a tricky one to name perfectly since insurers in both Canada and the US like to be 'unique'." — John Whaley, #beta_insurance_usa
Suggested Opportunity
Context-sensitive help text per insurer (e.g. "For Aetna, this is the 10-digit number starting with W on the front of your card") would reduce wrong-number submissions from patients.
Patient
Task
Save and continue — or abandon partway through
▶
Pain Point
If a patient starts the insurance section but doesn't finish and a staff member later opens the same in-progress form to complete it on their behalf, the insurance section is silently skipped — all data the patient entered is lost and no policy is created
Community"Once a staff member takes over an in-progress form that the patient started, the system skips the insurance section entirely, so the data the patient entered is lost and the claim item isn't created." — Crystal J., #support_clinical_forms (Bug CFORM-2316)
Opportunity
Fix CFORM-2316: preserve patient-entered insurance data when staff completes an in-progress form, or surface a clear warning that the insurance section will be skipped before the staff member proceeds.
Step 1.3
Patient uploads front & back of insurance card
Patient
Task
Photograph or upload their physical insurance card
▶
Pain Point
Card images sometimes fail to attach to the policy — they disappear silently (not visible in the completed form or Files section) with no error shown to the patient or clinic
Community"Insurance card photos NOT showing up in insurance policy. Images are also not showing up in completed intake form. Completed form does show 'Both sides' and member # but the images are absent." — Amy Allan, #code-patient-insurance-upload
Opportunity
AI-powered card scanning (OCR) to extract Member ID, group number, and insurer name from the uploaded card image would eliminate manual entry entirely and reduce wrong-number submissions.
Community"If we could ingest the artifacts during intake and turn them into structured data on the patient record, either in a chart entry or on the face sheet, would be fire." — Jeremy, #jane-ai-sdk
Jane System
Task
Store card images and create policy in "Awaiting Review" state
▶
Pain Point
Activity logging records policy creation at "Save and Continue" — before the intake form is fully submitted — which can create phantom activity log entries that mislead support staff investigating policy issues
Community"We actually record this info early, i.e. when the patient hits Save and Continue for the archived Claim that is waiting until the Intake Form is submitted. Do we want to maintain this activity logging?" — Spencer, #pt-insurance-foundations
2
Staff Reviews & Approves Policy
Step 2.1
Staff sees policy flagged as "Awaiting Review"
Clinic Admin
Task
Find and open the pending policy in the patient's billing tab
▶
Pain Point
There is no centralized "policies awaiting review" queue — staff must check individual patient profiles or know to look in the billing notices area, making it easy for pending policies to sit unreviewed before the appointment
Community"Storing Insurance info in Patient profile notes because navigating to Billing → Insurance Policies mid-workflow is too slow. Workarounds on top of workarounds." — Maddie Maerz, #ai-sdk-hack-cad-portal-submission
Opportunity
A dedicated "Policies awaiting review" queue — filterable by appointment date — would let admins process all pending policies for the next day's patients in one pass instead of hunting across profiles.
Step 2.2
Staff verifies policy details against the insurance card
Clinic Admin
Task
Cross-check member ID, group number, and insurer against the uploaded card image
▶
Pain Point
If the patient uploaded card images but they failed to attach, staff have nothing to verify against — they must contact the patient again to re-collect the information, adding friction on both sides
Suggested Opportunity
Automated cross-checking between typed policy fields and extracted card data (via OCR) could flag mismatches during review rather than requiring manual comparison.
Clinic Admin
Task
Confirm the patient selected the correct insurer from the clinic's list
▶
Pain Point
Because insurer names in Jane don't always match what's on the card, wrong insurer selection is common — and at this review step there is no indication of whether the selected insurer's name matches the uploaded card
Suggested Opportunity
Display the insurer name from the uploaded card image (OCR-extracted) next to the Jane insurer selection so staff can instantly confirm a match during review.
Step 2.3
Staff approves or corrects & saves policy
Clinic Admin
Task
Approve the policy — or correct wrong fields before approving
▶
Pain Point
Once approved, the insurer field is locked — if staff notice a wrong insurer only after approving, they must archive the policy, losing the uploaded card images, and start over
Feedback"Once an insurance policy is created in Jane, the insurer is locked in... the only fix is to archive the policy and start over from scratch, losing the patient's uploaded insurance card." — Maddie Maerz, #proj-changeinsurer
Opportunity
The active #proj-changeinsurer project addresses this — allowing insurer to be changed post-approval via a single guided flow without losing policy history or card images.
Jane System
Task
Mark policy as "Active" and make it available for billing
▶
Suggested Opportunity
After approval, surface a prompt to attach the policy to the patient's upcoming appointment — closing the loop between policy creation and billing readiness in a single action.
3
Attach Policy to Appointment
Step 3.1
Staff manually attaches the approved policy to the appointment
Clinic Admin
Task
Navigate to the appointment and add the patient's insurance policy
▶
Pain Point
An active insurance policy on a patient profile does not automatically attach to their appointment — staff must manually add it every time, even for returning patients with unchanged coverage
Community"Why does Jane make me add the insurance policies manually every time — doesn't she know Mike has insurance coverage by now?" — John Whaley, #ai-sdk-hack-eli-1-0
Opportunity
Auto-attach the patient's active insurance policy to new appointments when there is only one open policy on file — with an easy override if the policy has changed. This eliminates the single most common source of "forgot to attach insurance" billing delays.
Clinic Admin
Task
Use "Copy previous" at booking to carry over insurance details from the last appointment
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Surface the "Copy previous insurance" option more prominently at the booking step — or make it the default for returning patients — rather than requiring staff to know the feature exists.
Step 3.2
Policy is confirmed on appointment — ready to bill
Jane System
Task
Display insurance info on the appointment panel, confirm billing is ready
▶
Suggested Opportunity
An "Insurance ready" indicator on the appointment card in the schedule view — green if policy is attached and active, amber if awaiting review or missing — would let admins triage at a glance during busy check-in periods.
Clinic Admin
Task
Confirm patient's digital insurance upload worked — or collect paper card at front desk as fallback
▶
Pain Point
When card images fail silently during intake, staff only discover the gap at the appointment — forcing them to re-scan or re-photograph the card in person, defeating the purpose of digital intake
Feedback"Digital insurance policy upload — no more tracking you down for ID numbers." — Patient review about switching to Jane, #staffers
4
Alternate Path: Staff Enters Policy Manually
Step 4.1
Staff creates a new insurance policy directly on the patient profile
Clinic Admin
Task
Navigate to patient Billing tab > Insurance Policies > Add new policy
▶
Pain Point
Staff cannot access the insurance section of an intake form — they can only add policies manually via the Billing tab. This means if a staff member takes over a patient's in-progress intake, insurance info is silently lost and must be re-entered manually
Community"Staff members don't have access to complete the Insurance Info section of an intake form because they can enter that information manually from the patient's Billing tab." — Crystal J., #support_clinical_forms
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter all policy details and scan/upload the insurance card themselves
▶
Pain Point
Manually re-entering insurance data that the patient already filled into the intake form is pure double-handling — a common occurrence when the digital upload fails or staff takes over the form
Suggested Opportunity
Even without full OCR, allowing staff to photograph the insurance card and auto-populate the policy fields during manual entry would reduce the transcription burden at the front desk.
Step 4.2
Policy is saved as Active (no review required for staff-entered policies)
Jane System
Task
Create policy as Active — skipping the Awaiting Review state
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Staff-entered policies skip the review queue, which is correct — but there is no visual distinction between a "staff-verified" policy and a "patient-submitted and staff-reviewed" policy. A policy provenance label could help in auditing and support investigations.
