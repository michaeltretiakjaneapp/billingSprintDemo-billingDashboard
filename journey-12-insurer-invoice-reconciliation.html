Insurer Invoice / Reconciliation

The end-of-period billing view: a biller pulling together what was submitted to a specific insurer, what was paid, what was denied, what is still outstanding, and reconciling the numbers so the clinic understands its true receivables position with that payer. Covers report generation, aging analysis, discrepancy investigation, and the external workarounds billers rely on when Jane's reporting falls short. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #support_us_manualtherapy, and billing ride-along notes.

G1 Biller can see billed vs paid vs outstanding vs denied for any insurer in any period without leaving Jane or opening a spreadsheet
G2 Claims aging past 30/60/90 days without a response are automatically surfaced as an action queue
G3 Every dollar discrepancy (underpayment, contractual variance, unapplied ERA) is traceable to a specific claim

Trigger the Reconciliation
Step 0.1
Biller decides to review what a specific insurer owes the clinic
Biller
Task
Decide to run an insurer-specific reconciliation — triggered by month-end close, a suspected underpayment, or a claim that's been outstanding too long
▶
Pain Point
There is no proactive alert or scheduled prompt in Jane telling a biller that a reconciliation is overdue or that claims are aging without response. The decision to reconcile is entirely reactive — billers only do it when something feels wrong or when month-end forces the issue. Many clinics never do it systematically at all.
Feedback"We know there's probably money sitting out there with insurers that we haven't chased, but we don't have a system to find it. We just deal with what comes back to us." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A recurring "AR health check" prompt — surfaced at the start of each month — showing each insurer's outstanding balance and oldest unresponded claim age would make proactive reconciliation the default behaviour rather than the exception.
Biller
Task
Navigate to the billing report or insurer invoice section in Jane to pull the relevant data
▶
Pain Point
There is no dedicated "insurer reconciliation" workflow in Jane. Billers piece together the picture from multiple disconnected places: the Billing tab (for submitted/outstanding claims), ERA history (for what was paid), and the billing report export. No single view shows all of billed, paid, denied, and outstanding for one insurer in one period.
Community"To figure out what Aetna owes us I have to look at the billing tab, pull an export, and cross-reference it with our ERA folder. It takes an hour and I'm never confident I've got the full picture." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
An insurer-scoped AR summary view — filterable by payer and date range, showing billed / paid / adjusted / denied / outstanding as a single dashboard row per insurer — is the core missing capability that this journey requires. It is the prerequisite for everything else in this flow.
1
Generate the Report
Step 1.1
Biller runs a billing report filtered to the target insurer and date range
Biller
Task
Set report filters: insurer, date range (DOS or submission date), claim status, practitioner
▶
Pain Point
Jane's billing report filters are limited — billers can filter by practitioner and date range but cannot easily filter by insurer and claim status simultaneously in a single report view. Getting a clean "all claims for Insurer X in Q1 that are still outstanding" requires multiple filter passes and manual cross-referencing.
Community"I want to see all submitted claims for Blue Cross that haven't had a response yet. I can't do that in one step in Jane — I have to filter, export, and then filter again in Excel." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Compound filtering on the billing tab — insurer AND status AND date range — without requiring an export step would make insurer-specific reconciliation queries answerable directly in Jane in under 30 seconds.
Jane System
Task
Generate the report — query all matching claims and return the results
▶
Pain Point
The billing report times out on large datasets. Clinics with high claim volumes or long date ranges regularly hit a timeout before the report finishes generating — forcing them to narrow the date range, run multiple partial reports, and stitch results together manually. This is a known and reported issue.
Community"Every time I try to pull a full-year billing report it times out. I've had to break it into quarters and then combine the CSVs myself. It's tedious and I worry I'm double-counting something." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
Async report generation — where Jane queues the report, sends the biller a notification when it's ready, and stores it for download — would eliminate timeouts entirely and allow large historical reports without the current workaround of splitting by date range.
Step 1.2
Biller reviews the report output — what was billed, what was paid, what is still open
Biller
Task
Read through the report to understand the insurer's overall payment behaviour in the period
▶
Pain Point
Jane's billing report export is a flat claim-level list with no aggregation — no subtotals by insurer, no billed vs paid summary, no denial rate calculation. Every summary metric must be computed manually in Excel using SUMIF formulas or pivot tables. Billers who aren't spreadsheet-proficient simply can't do this analysis.
Feedback"I export the CSV and then spend an hour building pivot tables to get the numbers I need. My front desk staff can't do this at all — it requires someone who knows Excel well." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A summary row at the top of the billing report (or a separate "AR summary" view) showing total billed, total paid, total adjusted, total denied, and total outstanding — aggregated per insurer — would give billers the headline numbers without requiring any external analysis.
Biller
Task
Identify claims where ERA payment does not match the expected contracted rate
▶
Pain Point
Distinguishing a contractual adjustment (the insurer paid the correct contracted rate, which is less than billed) from an underpayment (the insurer paid less than the contracted rate) requires knowing the contracted fee schedule — which lives outside Jane. Jane shows what was paid but not whether it was the correct amount per contract, making underpayments invisible without a separate fee schedule lookup.
Community"I can see what they paid but I don't know if that's what they're supposed to pay. I have to look up our fee schedule separately for every code to check if they underpaid us." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Per-insurer contracted rate configuration in Jane — allowing billers to enter expected payment amounts per CPT code — would enable Jane to automatically flag underpayments (paid amount < contracted rate) and surface them as a "contract variance" category in the reconciliation view.
2
Aging Analysis — Find What's Overdue
Step 2.1
Biller identifies claims submitted 30 / 60 / 90+ days ago with no ERA response
Biller
Task
Filter the billing report by submission date to find claims older than 30 days with no payment recorded
▶
Pain Point
There is no claim aging view in Jane. A claim submitted 90 days ago with no ERA response looks identical in the billing tab to one submitted yesterday — both show as "Submitted." The only way to identify aging claims is to export the billing report and calculate days since submission in Excel.
Community"I have no idea how old my outstanding claims are unless I export them and do the math myself. Jane should show me a 30/60/90 day aging bucket — that's basic AR management." — Canny feature request, Billing
Opportunity
An AR aging view — claims bucketed by days outstanding (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+) filterable by insurer — is the single most requested missing feature in the billing workflow. It is standard in every dedicated practice management system and would immediately surface the claims most at risk of timely filing expiry.
Biller
Task
Distinguish between "no acknowledgement received" vs "acknowledged but payer hasn't responded" vs "denied and not yet resubmitted"
▶
Pain Point
All three states — no 277 acknowledgement, acknowledged but no ERA yet, and denied but not resubmitted — appear as "Submitted" in Jane. They require completely different actions (investigate clearinghouse transmission, follow up with payer, correct and resubmit) but there is no way to distinguish them without opening each claim individually.
Community"I have 40 claims sitting in Submitted and I don't know which ones are just waiting on the payer, which ones never made it through the clearinghouse, and which ones I need to resubmit. I have to click into each one." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Sub-status labels within "Submitted" — "Awaiting Acknowledgement," "Acknowledged — Pending Payment," "Denied — Action Required" — would let billers immediately filter to the right action queue without clicking into individual claims.
Step 2.2
Biller follows up with the insurer on aging claims
Biller
Task
Check claim status by calling the insurer's provider line or logging into the insurer portal
▶
Pain Point
Checking claim status in Jane requires leaving the system entirely — calling the insurer's provider phone line (with hold times of 20–45 minutes) or logging into the insurer's web portal separately. There is no electronic claim status inquiry (276/277 transaction) integration in Jane — all follow-up is fully manual.
Community"Following up on outstanding claims means being on hold with the insurer for 30 minutes per claim. I can only do a handful per day. There has to be a better way." — Billing ride-along notes
Opportunity
276/277 Claim Status Inquiry integration — allowing billers to submit a claim status request from within Jane and receive the payer's current status electronically — would eliminate phone follow-up for the majority of status checks and let billers action dozens of aging claims in the time it currently takes to check one.
Biller
Task
Log the outcome of the follow-up call — claim status, expected payment date, or required correction
▶
Pain Point
There is no notes field or follow-up log on a claim in Jane. When a biller calls an insurer and is told "the claim is in process, expect payment in 10 business days," there is nowhere to record that information in Jane. The biller relies on memory, a sticky note, or an external spreadsheet — leading to repeat calls and no audit trail.
Community"I called about a claim last week and was told it's being processed. Now I can't remember which claim it was or what I was told. There's no place to write this stuff down in Jane." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A claim-level notes / activity log — where billers can record follow-up calls, payer responses, expected payment dates, and internal reminders — would create an audit trail and eliminate the reliance on external tracking for AR follow-up.
3
Reconcile Payments Against Claims
Step 3.1
Biller matches ERA payments received to the claims that were submitted
Biller
Task
Verify that every submitted claim has a corresponding ERA response recorded in Jane
▶
Pain Point
ERA auto-linking failures (where Jane attaches a remittance response to the wrong claim or leaves it unlinked) mean that some ERA payments are recorded in Jane but not credited to the correct claim. The reconciliation then shows that claim as unpaid even though the insurer did pay — the money is just attached to the wrong record.
Community"I have a claim showing as outstanding but I'm pretty sure we were paid for it. I just can't find where the payment ended up. It probably got linked to the wrong invoice." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A "find mislinked payments" tool — surfacing ERA responses that are linked to a claim for the wrong patient, wrong DOS, or wrong insurer — would make linking errors discoverable during reconciliation rather than requiring the biller to find them by accident.
Biller
Task
Reconcile EFT deposit amounts against ERA totals — confirm the bank received what Jane recorded
▶
Pain Point
The EFT amount that lands in the clinic's bank account must be matched to the ERA that explains it — but Jane does not have bank feed integration. Billers must manually compare bank statements against Jane's remittance records, looking for the matching deposit amount. When a single EFT covers multiple ERAs, or an ERA is split across two EFTs, the matching becomes time-consuming.
Community"The EFT came in for $3,240 but I have two ERAs from that insurer that week. I had to add them up manually to figure out which combination matches the bank deposit." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
EFT reference number entry on ERA records in Jane — matching the trace number on the 835 to the bank deposit — would allow billers to confirm "this deposit = this ERA" and complete the cash reconciliation loop without a separate spreadsheet.
Step 3.2
Biller categorises each outstanding balance: underpayment, denial, or in-flight
Biller
Task
For each claim with an outstanding balance, determine whether it is: an underpayment to appeal, a denial to correct and resubmit, or a legitimate in-flight claim still awaiting first response
▶
Pain Point
All three categories — underpayment, denial, in-flight — are indistinguishable at a glance in Jane's billing tab. Each requires a completely different action, but the biller must open every claim individually to determine which bucket it belongs to. For a clinic with hundreds of outstanding claims, categorising the full AR is a half-day task done entirely by hand.
Feedback"Reconciling our AR takes me most of a Friday every month. I'm basically auditing every open claim one at a time to figure out what action to take. It shouldn't be this manual." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
AI-assisted AR triage — classifying each outstanding claim based on its ERA response history, denial codes, and age — could pre-categorise the AR queue into "appeal," "correct and resubmit," and "awaiting response" buckets, reducing a half-day manual task to a 15-minute review.
4
Produce Final Statement for Management
Step 4.1
Biller produces an end-of-period insurer summary for clinic ownership or management
Biller
Task
Build the insurer-level summary: total billed, total collected, collection rate, top denial reasons, outstanding AR balance
▶
Pain Point
Every end-of-period insurer summary is built outside Jane — typically a custom Excel workbook that the biller maintains separately. Jane's export data does not include all required fields (e.g. ERA payment date, ERA response code, resubmission history), so billers supplement with manual entries. When the biller leaves, the spreadsheet methodology leaves with them.
Feedback"My monthly insurer report is a spreadsheet I built myself two years ago. It's held together with duct tape. If I left, the next person would have no idea how it works." — Billing ride-along notes
Opportunity
A built-in insurer performance report in Jane — showing collection rate, average days to payment, denial rate by code, and outstanding AR balance per insurer — would give clinic owners and billers a consistent, repeatable view without relying on biller-built spreadsheets. This feeds directly into Journey #13 (Insurer Reporting & Analytics).
Biller
Task
Share the summary with the clinic owner and flag any insurers where the collection rate or payment speed has deteriorated
▶
Pain Point
Clinic owners who are not billers have no way to access insurer performance data directly in Jane — they are entirely dependent on their biller to produce and interpret it. If the biller is busy, sick, or leaves, the owner has no visibility into the clinic's AR health with any given insurer.
Feedback"The clinic owner asks me how we're doing with our insurers and I have to pull it together for them. They can't see any of this themselves in Jane." — Billing ride-along notes
Suggested Opportunity
A read-only "AR overview" dashboard accessible to clinic owners (without full biller permissions) — showing each insurer's current outstanding balance, recent collection rate, and any aging claims — would give owners independent visibility into their revenue cycle health.
Step 4.2
Biller generates a formal insurer invoice if required by the payer
Biller
Task
Some insurers (particularly Canadian provincial plans or self-insured employers) require a formal invoice listing all claims in the period — generate and submit this
▶
Pain Point
Jane has no formal "insurer invoice" generation feature — a structured document listing all claims billed to a specific insurer in a period, formatted for submission to the payer. Clinics that work with insurers requiring this (e.g. WCB, ICBC, MVA, employer group plans) generate these invoices manually in Word or a separate tool and paste in data from Jane's export.
Community"WCB requires us to send them a monthly invoice with all our claims listed. I export from Jane, copy the data into a Word template, and format it manually every month. Takes about 2 hours." — Support thread, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A configurable insurer invoice template — where the clinic defines the required fields, layout, and submission format per insurer — would automate the monthly invoice generation for payers that require formal billing statements.
