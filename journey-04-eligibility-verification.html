Eligibility Verification

How a biller or admin confirms a patient has active coverage and understands their benefits before an appointment — from running the electronic check through interpreting the response, identifying gaps, and calling the insurer when the electronic data falls short. Signal from #ai-sdk-hack-eli-1-0, #support_usa_billing, #billing_community_posts, #jane_community_posts_fb, and #pt-billing-service.

G1 Biller can confidently confirm coverage and collect the right patient portion before the visit
G2 Electronic check answers the key benefit questions without requiring a phone call to the insurer
G3 When a phone call is needed, the biller already has the exact questions and patient details ready to go

Many billers skip the electronic check entirely and go straight to calling — because past experience has taught them the electronic response won't have what they need for their specific payer or discipline

Suggested Opportunity
A per-payer "electronic coverage quality" indicator — showing what categories of benefit data this insurer typically returns — would help billers set expectations before running a check, reducing wasted clicks.

1
Run the Electronic Eligibility Check
Step 1.1
Biller triggers eligibility check in Jane via ClaimMD
Biller
Task
Select service type / discipline and run the check
▶
Pain Point
New billers have no frame of reference for what a "good" eligibility response looks like — when expected benefit data is missing, they assume something is broken rather than understanding the insurer simply didn't return it
Community"She assumed the results would look a certain way, and when Jane displayed the data differently, she thought something might be wrong with the setup — especially since the PT coverage details she expected to see weren't included." — Nicky, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
A brief inline explainer when running the first eligibility check — setting expectations about what electronic responses can and can't return — would reduce false support escalations from new billers.
Jane System
Task
Send 270 request via ClaimMD and return the 271 response
▶
Pain Point
Not all insurers support electronic eligibility — and those that do return wildly inconsistent data across payers and disciplines, making the feature feel unreliable even when it's working correctly
Community"Verification detail is limited: 2 customers clarified that ClaimMD's eligibility data isn't unusually detailed — but it's standard across clearinghouses." — Community post summary, #jane_community_posts_fb
Step 1.2
Jane displays the raw 271 eligibility response
Biller
Task
Scroll through the raw eligibility response to find the relevant benefit details
▶
Pain Point
The raw response is dense, unfiltered, and not organized around the questions a biller actually needs answered — copay, deductible, OOP max, visit limits — forcing them to hunt through noise to find signal
Community"When I do a check the way you describe in Jane, it comes up with a deductible and copay and a bunch of stuff about colonoscopy. I don't know why that's listed under psychotherapy. It also says no copay." — Clinic, via #support_usa_billing
Pain Point
The same data field can show conflicting values depending on the payer — Jane showed $0 copay for a patient while Availity showed $20, with no explanation of why the values differ
Community"In Jane it lists that the patient doesn't have a copay, whereas Availity does communicate the $20 copay. There are different amounts being communicated — $0 from ClaimMD vs $20 from Availity." — Sabrina, #support_usa_billing
Opportunity
The "Eli" AI hackathon project (John Whaley, Mike Tretiak) built an AI-powered eligibility summary surfaced directly on the appointment panel — discipline-aware, pre-loaded with the key questions, and transparent about what was and wasn't answered. This is the clearest near-term opportunity in this journey.
2
Interpret the Eligibility Results
Step 2.1
Biller extracts the key benefit numbers they need
Biller
Task
Find copay, deductible remaining, OOP max, coinsurance, and visit limits
▶
Pain Point
Jane doesn't track real-time deductible accumulation — billers must manually note what's been met from the eligibility check and manually update the patient portion in Jane each time
Community"Jane isn't able to pull real-time deductible accumulation or automatically update the patient portion once the deductible is met. That piece will need to be managed manually by the clinic." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Even without real-time sync, storing the last-known deductible accumulation from the most recent eligibility check — and flagging it as of a specific date — would reduce the manual tracking burden and give billers a starting point.
Biller
Task
Identify whether the response is discipline-specific or generic plan-level data
▶
Pain Point
Generic plan-level responses often include irrelevant benefit categories (colonoscopy listed under a psychotherapy check) while missing the discipline-specific data the biller actually needs — there's no separation or signal
Community"The checks come from the insurer directly, just via the clearinghouse. If they're not helpful, that's likely meaning that the insurers specifically are not providing very helpful information." — Mosh, #billing_community_posts
Opportunity
The Eli AI summary is discipline-aware — filtering to the practitioner's specialty (chiro, PT, acupuncture, behavioral health) and stripping out irrelevant plan-level noise. This is the core design bet of the hackathon project.
Step 2.2
Biller identifies what's missing from the response
Biller
Task
Determine which benefit questions were not answered electronically
▶
Pain Point
There is no clear distinction in the current response between "this insurer doesn't cover this" vs "this insurer just didn't return this data" — both look like absence, but one requires a phone call and the other doesn't
Community"Electronic eligibility checks have real limits — a lot of payers won't return service-level detail electronically, only over the phone." — John Whaley, #ai-sdk-hack-eli-1-0
Opportunity
The Eli AI summary explicitly distinguishes "answered with confidence," "not covered," and "gap — needs phone verification" — surfacing this three-state model is the single biggest UX improvement available in the eligibility check flow.
Biller
Task
Check for carve-outs, prior auth requirements, or behavioral health payer differences
▶
Pain Point
Carve-out plans — where a plan's mental health benefits are administered by a separate payer (e.g. Optum, not Aetna) — are easy to miss in the raw response. Claims sent to the wrong payer will deny with no obvious reason shown
Community"Benefits are administered by Optum Behavioral Health (not Aetna) — this is a high-priority flag since claims sent to Aetna will deny." — John Whaley, Eli test case description
Opportunity
The Eli AI summary surfaces carve-out detection as a high-priority flag — "benefits administered by [other payer]" shown prominently so billers catch it before submitting to the wrong insurer.
Step 2.3
Biller sets the patient portion in Jane based on what was found
Biller
Task
Manually update copay and patient responsibility fields on the policy or appointment
▶
Pain Point
All benefit data from the eligibility check must be manually transcribed into Jane — there is no way to "apply" the results of a check to a patient's policy or appointment; every number must be typed in separately
Suggested Opportunity
A "apply these benefits" action from the eligibility summary — pre-filling copay, coinsurance, and deductible fields on the patient's policy — would eliminate the manual transcription step that currently follows every successful check.
3
Call the Insurer (When Electronic Falls Short)
Step 3.1
Biller prepares to call the insurer for missing benefit details
Biller
Task
Collect patient details, provider info, and policy numbers needed before dialing
▶
Pain Point
All the context needed for the call — patient name, DOB, member ID, provider NPI, group number — must be gathered manually from different parts of Jane before dialing. There is no "call prep" state in the tool
Community"The script includes the patient and policy details they'd otherwise have to hunt for in different spots across Jane." — John Whaley, #ai-sdk-hack-eli-1-0
Opportunity
The Eli hackathon build generates a ready-to-use phone script from the gap list — the exact questions to ask, pre-loaded with patient and provider details. When a call is unavoidable, the work should already be done before the biller picks up the phone.
Biller
Task
Find the correct phone number for the insurer's provider services line
▶
Pain Point
For some payers, even the phone number on the eligibility response doesn't match the correct provider services line — billers have to search outside Jane to find the right number
Community"Would it be possible to show phone numbers alongside each insurance option in the dropdown? Since there can be multiple numbers depending on the plan, it might be helpful to quickly confirm we're calling the same contact Health Harbor is using." — Kayla, #healthharbor-janeapp
Step 3.2
Biller calls the insurer and collects missing benefit details
Biller
Task
Verify active coverage, remaining deductible, copay, visit limits, and prior auth requirements by phone
▶
Pain Point
For disciplines like hospice, carve-out behavioral health, or niche specialties, even a phone verification may be insufficient — certain benefits require pre-authorization and the call itself is just the start of a longer process
Community"In hospice care, an eligibility response may or may not confirm specifically if hospice is a covered benefit. Often, this benefit requires pre-authorization and a call to the insurance to verify benefits." — Audelia, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
An AI-assisted note-taker that listens during the benefits verification call and automatically captures the answers into the patient's policy fields would eliminate the post-call data entry step entirely.
Step 3.3
Biller records call results and updates patient policy in Jane
Biller
Task
Manually transcribe the verified benefits into the patient's policy and appointment
▶
Pain Point
Phone-verified benefit data — deductible remaining, visit count, specific CPT coverage — must be manually entered into Jane with no structured field for "source: phone verification on [date]". Data staleness has no tracking mechanism
Suggested Opportunity
A "phone verification" benefit entry mode — with a timestamp and "verified by phone" tag — would distinguish phone-collected data from electronic data in the policy record, making it easier to know when to re-verify.
Biller
Task
Confirm patient is ready to be seen — notify front desk if any coverage issues need addressing before the visit
▶
Suggested Opportunity
A "benefits verified" status flag on the appointment — with a link to the last eligibility check and any phone notes — would give the front desk a single place to confirm billing readiness before the patient arrives.
