Insurance Onboarding — First-Time Setup

The "zero to billing" journey for a clinic enabling insurance in Jane for the first time. Covers enabling the billing mode, configuring organization and staff billing details, setting up a clearinghouse, getting credentialed, and submitting the first claim. Signal from #support_usa_billing, #ask_asc_anything, #pt-insurance-setup, and #win_loss_insights.

G1 Clinic can confidently enable insurance billing in Jane without needing to call for help at every step
G2 Billing details (NPI, taxonomy, group vs individual) are configured correctly before the first claim leaves Jane
G3 Clearinghouse is connected and credentialed so claims route correctly from day one
0
Decide & Enable Insurance
Step 0.1
Clinic decides to start billing insurance in Jane
Account Owner
Task
Evaluate whether to use Jane's insurance billing or a separate billing system
▶
Pain Point
Clinics transitioning from another system (e.g. AcuSimple, SimplePractice) often have a biller who is resistant to switching — the AO is eager but the person who actually does billing is not
Feedback"The biller is hesitant about switching, but the owner is eager to move over to Jane as they're starting a new wellness practice." — US Insurance ASC, #ask_asc_anything
Suggested Opportunity
A clear comparison page or guided discovery call positioning for switchers (not just new billers) could reduce early churn from biller resistance.
Account Owner
Task
Book insurance onboarding support (ASC, insurance onboarding service, or billing service)
▶
Pain Point
Multiple support pathways exist (free ASC, complimentary Insurance Account Setup Service, $299 Dedicated Insurance Onboarding, Jane Billing Service) but clinics often don't know which one they need or qualify for
Community"It sounds like they could benefit from focused insurance billing training. I'm going to tag in the insurance team, as I believe they offer a dedicated insurance onboarding service for clinics new to medical billing or looking for additional guidance." — Sarah Earle, #ask_asc_anything
Pain Point
Clinics who sign up and start paying before going live spend months paying for Jane without being functional — leading to significant frustration and early churn risk
Feedback"Paying for Jane for several months before fully going live added to their frustration, as the setup process took longer than expected." — Kelsey, #win_loss_insights
Opportunity
A proactive outreach trigger for new accounts that have enabled insurance but haven't submitted a claim within 30 days could surface the right support option before the clinic goes silent or churns.
Step 0.2
Enable insurance billing mode in Settings
Account Owner
Task
Toggle US Insurance on in Settings › Billing
▶
Pain Point
The US Insurance toggle and the per-insurer insurer type must match — but there is nothing in the enable flow that explains this dependency or prompts the clinic to verify
Community"There are a couple of things to consider: insurance type, US insurance = enabled." — Mosh, internal discussion
Suggested Opportunity
A guided "enable insurance" flow (rather than a single toggle) could walk the clinic through the key dependencies — billing mode, insurer type, NPI, clearinghouse — before they submit their first claim.
Jane System
Task
Unlock insurance billing features across the account
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Post-enable, surface a "Setup Checklist" in the UI listing the remaining steps (NPIs, billing codes, clearinghouse) with completion state — so clinics know what's left before they're ready to bill.
1
Configure Billing Details
Step 1.1
Set up organization / location billing info
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter group/organization NPI and Tax ID in Settings › Locations
▶
Pain Point
Clinics frequently conflate group NPI (Type 2, goes in Settings › Locations) with individual NPI (Type 1, goes in Staff › Billing) — a misconfiguration that causes Box 33 / Box 24J errors on claims
Community"In Settings › Locations. This is where Jane will pull from as the default for Box 32 and Box 33. In Staff › Billing settings, ideally they'll just have the type 1, individual NPI as the Rendering Provider Identifier." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Suggested Opportunity
Inline label distinguishing Type 1 (individual) vs Type 2 (organization) NPI at the point of entry — with a plain-language explanation of where each one appears on the CMS-1500 — would prevent the most common setup misconfiguration.
Step 1.2
Set up per-staff billing info
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter individual NPI and taxonomy code in Staff › Billing for each provider
▶
Pain Point
Taxonomy codes are a major sticking point — clinics don't know what they are, why they matter, or how to find them, leading to escalated support calls and initial claim rejections
Community"The clinic became quite frustrated and kept asking about why this impacts the 'taxonomy code' and asked what a 'taxonomy code' was." — US Billing Customer Support, #support_usa_billing
Pain Point
Group vs individual credentialing logic (who is the billing provider vs rendering provider) is confusing — especially for supervisee/supervisor scenarios and multi-discipline clinics
Community"The rendering provider NPI is meant to be the NPI of the clinician who rendered the service. The billing provider NPI depends on how the clinic is contracted with the insurer — individual vs group." — Emily, Support
Opportunity
A guided staff billing setup wizard — with discipline-aware defaults, taxonomy code lookup, and clear explanations of which box each field maps to — could eliminate the #1 category of new clinic setup errors.
Step 1.3
Set up billing codes & session types
Clinic Admin
Task
Add CPT/billing codes and link them to session types
▶
Pain Point
Clinics that don't discover the "default billing codes" feature end up manually adding CPT codes to every appointment — a time-consuming workflow that causes errors and is the main source of early billing frustration
Feedback"Staff are manually verifying CPT codes and insurance policies for each session, leading to time-consuming and error-prone workflows. We hopefully mitigated this by going over how to add default billing codes to session types." — Kelsey, #win_loss_insights
Opportunity
A nudge or onboarding prompt when a clinic submits their first claim without default billing codes set up could redirect them to the feature before the manual workflow becomes a habit.
2
Clearinghouse Setup
Step 2.1
Sign up for a clearinghouse account
Clinic Admin
Task
Create a ClaimMD account (or register for Stedi pilot)
▶
Pain Point
Clinics transitioning from another system sometimes find their Tax ID / EIN already linked to that system's clearinghouse account — blocking them from registering under Jane
Community"When Connie tried registering with Claim.MD, she got an error saying their clinic number is already linked with SimplePractice, so they can't use it. What's the next step here?" — US Insurance ASC, #ask_asc_anything
Opportunity
The Stedi pilot aims to remove the need to log into a separate clearinghouse portal entirely — a fully embedded signup flow inside Jane would eliminate this class of problem for new clinics.
Clearinghouse
Task
Provision clinic account and issue SFTP / API credentials
▶
Pain Point
ClaimMD credentials must be created in the clearinghouse portal and then manually entered into Jane — a context-switch that is confusing and error-prone for non-technical clinic admins
Community"A user needs to update a bunch of settings and get logins and credentials in the Claim.MD portal before bringing those over to Jane." — Spencer, #proj-insurance-ai
Step 2.2
Connect clearinghouse to Jane
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter clearinghouse SFTP credentials into Jane's clearinghouse settings
▶
Pain Point
SFTP credential entry is technical and opaque — there is no validation feedback in Jane to confirm the connection is working until claims are actually sent
Community"We have an influx of customer reports (~25) stating their Claim.MD integration keeps dropping the SFTP connection, repeatedly requesting a reset." — Crystal J., #pt-insurance
Opportunity
An inline connection test ("Send a test ping to your clearinghouse") at setup would give clinics immediate confirmation that their credentials work before they submit real claims.
Jane System
Task
Store credentials and establish SFTP connection to clearinghouse
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Persistent connection health monitoring with a status indicator in the clearinghouse settings page would surface drops proactively instead of after claims fail to transmit.
Step 2.3
Enroll with payers in clearinghouse portal
Clinic Admin
Task
Complete payer enrollment for each insurer in ClaimMD (group and/or individual profiles)
▶
Pain Point
Payer enrollment is done in the clearinghouse portal — not in Jane — and requires setting up group vs individual billing profiles correctly per payer. Errors here only surface as claim rejections weeks later
Community"Under Prov Enrollment, he only has his individual profile set up. He'll want to set up a group profile and do the enrollments under the group profile for BCBS." — Katie, #support_usa_billing
Pain Point
Medicare credentialing can take 1–2 weeks — clinics are often surprised to discover they can't immediately bill Medicare after connecting their clearinghouse
Community"There may be some administrative lift up front to switch clearinghouses — credentialing with Medicare can take a week or two." — Mosh, Stedi pitch to Billing Service
Suggested Opportunity
A payer enrollment status view inside Jane (pulling from clearinghouse enrollment state) would let billers see which payers are ready without logging into the clearinghouse portal.
3
Submit the First Claim
Step 3.1
Add insurance to first patient & appointment
Clinic Admin
Task
Create patient insurance policy and attach to appointment
▶
Pain Point
Clinics don't always realize they need to explicitly attach the insurance policy to the appointment — leading to claims that generate without insurance or require manual fix-up after the fact
Suggested Opportunity
A pre-appointment insurance readiness check ("This patient has a policy — confirm it's attached to this appointment") at checkout could catch missing attachments before a claim is created.
Step 3.2
Create & submit the claim
Biller
Task
Review claim, verify billing codes, and submit
▶
Pain Point
First-claim rejections for NPI mismatches, missing taxonomy, or wrong billing provider are extremely common — the initial setup errors only become visible at this moment, often weeks into a clinic's billing journey
Community"Billing NPI is not on file, rendering NPI is not on file, Billing NPI is not authorized for Tax ID." — Real rejection code, #support_usa_billing
Pain Point
The challenges faced during first claims caused actual claim rejections and financial losses — which compounded stress for new clinic owners who were already overwhelmed with the transition
Feedback"The challenges initially faced caused claim rejections and financial losses, which added to already piling stress." — Kelsey, #win_loss_insights
Opportunity
A pre-submission claim validation step — checking that NPI, taxonomy, payer enrollment, and billing provider are all configured before the 837 is sent — could eliminate the majority of first-claim rejections for new clinics.
Jane System
Task
Generate 837 and transmit to clearinghouse
▶
Suggested Opportunity
A "first claim sent" in-app celebration moment — with a summary of what was submitted and what to expect next (timing, how to read the 277 acknowledgment) — would reduce the anxiety of not knowing whether it worked.
Step 3.3
Receive acknowledgment & assess result
Biller
Task
Check claim status — accepted, rejected, or pending
▶
Pain Point
New billers don't know how to interpret claim status codes — error messages like "invalid rendering provider taxonomy code" are cryptic and cause escalated support calls with no clear self-serve resolution path
Community"The clinic became quite frustrated and kept asking about why this impacts the 'taxonomy code' — they were getting stuck in a loop about why the note was coming up at all for the claim, even though it gets pushed through." — US Billing Customer Support
Opportunity
Plain-language explanations of the top 10 first-claim rejection codes — displayed inline next to the rejection in Jane — would allow most clinics to self-serve their first rejection fix without calling support.
Clinic Admin
Task
Celebrate first successful claim — or escalate for help if rejected
▶
Pain Point
Clinics that don't have access to billing specialist support at this moment (e.g. came in before proactive outreach was set up) often repeat the same mistake across multiple claims before discovering the fix
Feedback"The clinic would have benefited from earlier and more direct access to billing specialists, rather than uncovering billing pain points only after going live." — Kelsey, #win_loss_insights
Opportunity
A nudge in-product after the first claim is sent — offering a "have a billing expert review your setup" option — is an ideal moment to surface the Insurance Account Setup or Dedicated Onboarding Service for clinics that didn't take it before going live.
