Change Insurer on Existing Policy

Jane · Insurance Billing · Journey #14 — What happens when the wrong insurer is on a patient's policy and needs to be corrected. 188+ Canny votes. Active project: #proj-changeinsurer.

G1 Correct the wrong insurer quickly without disrupting existing claims
G2 Preserve the full paper trail — submission history, card images, EOBs
G3 Avoid duplicate submissions and clearinghouse rejections
G4 Keep the patient record clean and coherent after the correction
G5 Surface the right insurer for future appointments automatically
0
Discover the Error
Step 0.1
Wrong Insurer Identified
Biller
Task
Discovers wrong insurer on a submitted or pending claim
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The wrong insurer is most often discovered after submission — via a clearinghouse rejection, an ERA denial, or a patient calling to say their EOB came from the wrong plan. At this point the incorrect insurer may already be attached to multiple appointments and submitted claims.
Suggested Pain Point
There is no proactive insurer validation at claim creation — Jane does not warn if the insurer on the policy doesn't match payer data on file for this patient, or if the insurer type (CA vs US) is inconsistent with the clinic's billing mode. The error only surfaces downstream.
Suggested Opportunity
A pre-claim validation that cross-checks the patient's insurer against the clinic's active payer list and flags likely mismatches before submission — catching the error at the cheapest possible point in the lifecycle.
Clinic Admin
Task
Identifies the error during check-in or patient contact
▶
Suggested Pain Point
A patient arrives and mentions their insurance changed — or the admin scans their card and sees it doesn't match what's in Jane. At this moment, there is no streamlined way to update the insurer on the existing policy. The admin must decide between leaving the error in place and dealing with it later, or beginning the archive/recreate workaround immediately before the appointment.
Suggested Opportunity
An "Update Insurance" shortcut on the check-in screen that opens the policy editor with the insurer field unlocked — enabling front-desk corrections without disrupting the appointment flow.
Patient
Task
Reports receiving an EOB or bill from the wrong insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Patients who discover the error via an unexpected EOB or billing statement often don't understand what happened — they received a document from an insurer they may not recognise, creating confusion and eroding trust in the clinic. The clinic must absorb this patient-facing fallout in addition to the administrative correction.
Step 0.2
Assess the Scope of Impact
Biller
Task
Audits how many appointments and claims used the wrong insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
There is no quick way in Jane to see "all appointments and claims tied to this specific policy" at a glance. Billers must manually cross-reference the patient's appointment history, the Unsubmitted folder, and the Billing tab to reconstruct the full scope of the error — a time-consuming audit with no dedicated tooling.
Suggested Pain Point
If claims have already been submitted and paid under the wrong insurer, the biller must determine whether to void those claims, whether to issue a refund to the wrong payer, and how to resubmit to the correct insurer without triggering duplicate claim flags — a cascade of compliance decisions with no in-product guidance.
Suggested Opportunity
A policy impact summary on the correction flow: "This policy is linked to 6 appointments and 3 submitted claims. Here's what will be affected if you change the insurer" — giving the biller full situational awareness before making any changes.

Attempt the Change
Step 1.1
Navigate to the Policy
Clinic Admin
Task
Opens patient profile and locates the insurance policy
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The path to editing a policy is not obvious from the patient profile — admins must navigate through the correct tab and section to find the editable policy record. For staff unfamiliar with the billing area, this alone creates friction before the actual correction even begins.
Step 1.2
Discover the Field Is Locked
Clinic Admin
Task
Attempts to edit the insurer field — finds it is not editable
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Once a policy has been approved, the insurer field is locked and cannot be changed. This is the core blocker — 188+ Canny votes and the active #proj-changeinsurer project are a direct response to this single constraint. The lock exists for data integrity reasons, but the product provides no alternative path and no explanation of why it's locked or what to do instead.
Suggested Pain Point
There is no error message, warning, or help text explaining the lock. Admins discover it by trying to click the insurer dropdown and finding it unresponsive — with no indication that this is intentional or that a workaround exists.
Suggested Opportunity
At minimum, a locked field should show a tooltip: "Insurer cannot be changed after approval — contact your biller or use the policy correction workflow" — reducing the dead-end experience and pointing users toward a resolution path even before the native fix ships.
Jane System
Task
Enforces insurer field lock post-approval
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The lock applies even to Owners and Admins with full permissions — there is no elevated-permission override that lets a trusted user bypass the restriction in warranted cases. Every user, regardless of role, hits the same dead end.
Suggested Opportunity
A native "Change Insurer" action available to Owner/Admin roles that unlocks the field with an audit log entry — maintaining data integrity through traceability rather than through a blanket lock. This is the core request in #proj-changeinsurer.
2
The Workaround
Step 2.1
Archive the Original Invoice
Biller
Task
Archives (deactivates) the policy with the wrong insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Archiving the original policy is irreversible — it cannot be un-archived and edited. If the biller archives the wrong policy, or archives before capturing all necessary information, they lose access to the original record entirely.
Suggested Pain Point
Archiving destroys all uploaded insurance card images attached to the policy. The front and back of the insurance card — which took time to capture and upload — must be re-photographed or re-requested from the patient. This is one of the most commonly cited frustrations with the workaround.
Suggested Pain Point
The archived policy still appears in the patient's record but is greyed out — patients and admin staff who see the archived record are often confused about why there are two insurance entries for the same patient and which one is active.
Step 2.2
Recreate the Policy With Correct Insurer
Clinic Admin
Task
Creates a new policy record with the correct insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
All policy data — member ID, group number, relationship to insured, coordination of benefits order — must be manually re-entered from scratch. There is no "duplicate and edit" option; the new policy starts blank. In busy clinics this re-entry is error-prone and time-consuming.
Suggested Pain Point
Insurance card images must be re-uploaded since they were lost when the original policy was archived. If the patient isn't present, the admin must chase the patient for their card details again — creating a patient-facing friction point that stems entirely from a product limitation.
Suggested Opportunity
When a "Change Insurer" native action ships, carry forward all non-insurer fields (member ID, group number, relationship, card images, effective dates) to the corrected policy automatically — preserving everything except the specific field that was wrong.
Jane System
Task
Creates new policy record — disconnected from original
▶
Suggested Pain Point
The new policy has no linkage to the archived original — there is no "this replaces" relationship in the data model. Any claims, EOBs, or payment history tied to the original policy cannot be viewed alongside the new one. The patient's billing paper trail is permanently split across two disconnected records.
Suggested Opportunity
A policy correction history model: when a policy is corrected (insurer changed), the system preserves the full lineage — original policy → correction event → corrected policy — so the complete history is visible in one unified view rather than split across an archived and an active record.
3
Remediate Downstream Impact
Step 3.1
Re-attach to Appointments
Clinic Admin
Task
Re-links new policy to all affected appointments
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Because the new policy is a fresh record, it is not automatically linked to any past or future appointments. Every appointment that was previously attached to the wrong policy must be manually updated to use the new one — one by one, with no bulk re-attach capability.
Suggested Pain Point
Future appointments are also affected — recurring appointments that had the old policy attached will need to be updated individually. In practices with weekly recurring clients, this can mean dozens of appointments requiring manual re-attachment.
Suggested Opportunity
When a policy correction is made, offer a one-click "Re-attach to all appointments from [date range]" action — selecting which appointments should be updated in bulk rather than requiring per-appointment manual changes.
Step 3.2
Handle Previously Submitted Claims
Biller
Task
Voids claims submitted to wrong insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Claims submitted to the wrong insurer must be voided (Resubmission Code 8) to recover any payment made in error and remove the claim from the wrong payer's records. This is a multi-step process with real financial risk — accidental voids, missed timely filing on the correct insurer, and duplicate payment recovery all become possible failure modes.
Suggested Pain Point
If the wrong insurer has already paid the claim, the biller must issue a refund to the wrong payer before resubmitting to the correct one. Jane has no workflow for coordinating a payment refund alongside a claim void — billers manage this entirely outside the product.
Suggested Opportunity
A "Correct insurer and resubmit" guided workflow that handles the full sequence: void the claim to the wrong payer → flag for refund if paid → resubmit to the correct insurer — keeping the full correction traceable within Jane rather than split across external spreadsheets.
Biller
Task
Resubmits corrected claims to the right insurer
▶
Suggested Pain Point
Resubmitting to the correct insurer after a policy correction requires rebuilding the claim from scratch under the new policy — there is no "resubmit this claim under the corrected policy" shortcut. All claim data must be re-entered manually, and the timely filing clock on the correct insurer has been ticking since the date of service.
Suggested Pain Point
The corrected claim has no reference to the original claim submitted to the wrong insurer — there is no lineage visible in Jane. Auditors or payers who ask about claim history will see a gap that the clinic cannot easily explain from within the product.
4
Future State — Native Insurer Change
Step 4.1
Change Insurer In Place
Clinic Admin
Task
Uses native "Change Insurer" action on the existing policy
▶
Suggested Opportunity
An Owner/Admin-permissioned "Change Insurer" button on the policy that opens a modal with the insurer dropdown unlocked, an effective date selector, and a reason field for the audit log — allowing the correction to happen in place without archiving, without data loss, and without destroying the card images.
Suggested Opportunity
The change should write a timestamped audit log entry: "Insurer changed from [Old Insurer] to [New Insurer] by [User] on [Date] — Reason: [text]" — maintaining a complete correction history visible to all staff with billing access.
Jane System
Task
Validates the change and checks for downstream claim impact
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Before committing the insurer change, the system should surface a confirmation modal: "This policy is linked to 4 appointments and 2 unsubmitted claims. Changing the insurer will update all linked records. Do you want to also re-attach this policy to past appointments?" — giving the user informed control over the cascading effects.
Step 4.2
Handle Claims Under New Insurer
Biller
Task
Reviews which submitted claims need to be voided and resubmitted
▶
Suggested Opportunity
After the insurer change, a claim remediation queue that auto-identifies: (1) unsubmitted claims — update insurer automatically; (2) submitted/pending claims — prompt biller to void and resubmit; (3) paid claims — prompt biller to void, refund, and resubmit. Categorising the work rather than leaving the biller to audit it manually.
Suggested Opportunity
A "resubmit under corrected insurer" shortcut that rebuilds the claim with the new insurer pre-populated and flags it as a correction — eliminating the manual rebuild step and preserving the lineage between the original and corrected submissions.
Jane System
Task
Updates future appointments to use the corrected insurer
▶
Suggested Opportunity
All future appointments already linked to this policy should be automatically updated to reflect the corrected insurer — no per-appointment manual re-attachment required. Past appointments within a configurable date range should be offered as an optional bulk update, with the biller selecting which range applies.
