Insurer Management in Jane

Journey map covering how clinic admins and billers add, configure, and change insurers — from Settings setup through patient policy assignment and mid-lifecycle corrections. Grounded in signal from #support_usa_billing, #proj-changeinsurer, and #pt-insurance-foundations.

G1 Clinic can quickly add and correctly configure a new insurer in Settings
G2 Patients can accurately select their insurer during intake with no dead ends
G3 Staff can correct a wrong insurer on an existing policy without losing data or restarting from scratch
0
Add Insurer in Settings
Step 0.1
Navigate to Settings › Insurers
Clinic Admin
Task
Locate the Insurers page in Settings
▶
Pain Point
Clinics on certain plans or with restricted user roles can't find the Insurers tab at all — no clear message explains whether they need an add-on or a permission change
Community"Do all Jane accounts have the 'Insurers' tab in their settings even without the add-on? The customer says they don't have that option." — Casey M., Support
Suggested Opportunity
Surface a clear message when the Insurers tab is hidden due to plan or permissions — explain what's needed to unlock it rather than silently hiding the tab.
Jane System
Task
Display list of existing clinic insurers
▶
Pain Point
Clinics can accumulate 50+ insurers with near-identical names — duplicates form over time with no detection or merge tool
Community"Should these two insurers be the same single insurer? If they're the same, they might consider merging them." — Mosh, Support
Suggested Opportunity
Duplicate detection or a merge-insurer flow surfaced directly on the list view could help clinics keep their insurer roster clean over time.
Step 0.2
Click "Add Insurer" & fill in details
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter insurer name and internal label
▶
Pain Point
No naming guidance means clinics create ambiguous names that don't match what patients see on their cards — seeding wrong selections at intake
Community"I'd be sure to suggest to them that they name it something that stands out, so it's easy to spot as they are selecting the new insurer." — Karen, Billing Service
Suggested Opportunity
Inline naming tips or a payer lookup that pre-fills details from a known payer database could reduce ambiguous insurer names at creation time.
Clinic Admin
Task
Select insurer type: Canadian or US Insurance
▶
Pain Point
Insurer type (CA vs US) must match the clinic's billing mode setting — mismatches break claims silently and require SU-level batch intervention to fix
Community"US insurance doesn't work if the insurer isn't set up with the right insurer type, but also doesn't work if US insurance = enabled." — Mosh
Opportunity
Validate insurer type against billing mode at creation time — surface a clear warning if they don't match before the insurer is saved.
1
Configure Insurer Details
Step 1.1
Enter Payer ID & billing fields
Clinic Admin
Task
Enter the payer ID for electronic claim routing
▶
Pain Point
When a payer ID changes, clinics face a painful choice: update the insurer (risks re-routing all historical claims) or create a duplicate insurer and manually update every affected patient policy
Community"They unfortunately will need to add another insurer into their settings with this new payer ID and then manually go into each patient profile and add a new policy under this new insurer." — Karen, Billing Service
Opportunity
A payer ID versioning model (effective date on payer ID changes) would let clinics update a payer ID without breaking historical routing or forcing per-patient manual work.
Jane System
Task
Save insurer configuration and make it available clinic-wide
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Confirm save with a clear success state and a contextual "next step" prompt — e.g. reminding the admin to check staff-level billing overrides if applicable.
Step 1.2
Set up insurance-specific claim info (optional)
Clinic Admin
Task
Configure per-staff custom billing provider info for this insurer
▶
Pain Point
The distinction between group vs individual credentialing per-insurer is easy to misconfigure — leading to claim rejections that are hard to trace back to a billing setup issue
Community"If she's credentialed differently with BCBS than other insurers, she can use that Insurance Specific Claim Information area. If not, she can remove that!" — Katie, Support
Suggested Opportunity
A guided credentialing setup per insurer — individual vs group, NPI, taxonomy code — with contextual help text could reduce misconfiguration at this step.
2
Patient Selects Insurer at Intake
Step 2.1
Patient opens insurance section of intake form
Patient
Task
Browse insurer dropdown to find their insurance plan
▶
Pain Point
The dropdown only shows pre-configured insurers — patients whose insurer isn't listed hit a dead end with no way to proceed or enter a custom value
Community"I found a workaround of adding a question on the Questionnaire to enter an insurer not listed but I appreciate it's clunky and potentially confusing for patients." — Stine, Support
Pain Point
Clinics work around the missing "Other" option by adding a custom insurer named "Other" and editing intake instructions — a non-obvious admin configuration that most won't find on their own
Community"Settings > Insurers > Add one called Other... Custom Introduction: If you do not see your insurer, please select Other and type insurer name for the member ID." — Jeff H., Support
Opportunity
A native "Other / Not listed" option with a free-text field in the insurer dropdown would eliminate this dead end without requiring any clinic admin workaround. (Active Canny FR)
Jane System
Task
Render insurer dropdown from clinic's configured insurer list
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Fuzzy search or type-ahead on the insurer dropdown would reduce mis-selections in clinics with long insurer lists.
Step 2.2
Patient enters member ID, group number & submits
Patient
Task
Enter policy number, group number, upload insurance card
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Card scanning (OCR) to pre-fill member ID and group number from a photo of the insurance card would reduce manual entry errors and improve completion rates.
Jane System
Task
Create insurance policy on patient profile with insurer field locked
▶
Pain Point
Once a policy is created, the insurer is permanently locked — any error requires archiving the entire policy and starting over, destroying the uploaded insurance card image in the process
Feedback"Once an insurance policy is created in Jane, the insurer is locked in... the only fix is to archive the policy and start over from scratch, losing the patient's uploaded insurance card in the process." — Maddie Maerz, #proj-changeinsurer
3
Staff Assigns & Reviews Patient Policy
Step 3.1
Admin reviews patient policy after intake
Clinic Admin
Task
Open patient profile and verify insurer selection is correct
▶
Pain Point
With 50+ near-identical insurer names, wrong insurer selection is common — and there is no at-a-glance indicator on the policy card that might flag a mismatch before the first claim is submitted
Feedback"Clinics can have 50+ insurers with near-identical names... this was one of the first problems that stuck out to me while re-onboarding to the US space." — Maddie Maerz, PM
Suggested Opportunity
Highlight recently created or rarely-used insurers on a policy card to prompt admin review before the first claim is submitted.
Biller
Task
Confirm payer ID and billing info look correct before submitting claim
▶
Suggested Opportunity
A pre-submission summary showing the resolved payer ID and rendering provider for each claim would catch mismatches before they result in rejections.
Step 3.2
Biller submits claim to insurer
Biller
Task
Submit claim electronically via clearinghouse
▶
Pain Point
If the wrong insurer was set, a rejection only comes back weeks later — by then, multiple claims may have been submitted to the wrong payer, requiring significant manual cleanup
Community"They might need to update the patient policy because right now they were paid but an insurer not attached to the policy." — Erika, Support
Suggested Opportunity
Real-time eligibility check at claim submission could flag insurer mismatches before the 837 is transmitted — catching errors before the weeks-long feedback loop begins.
Jane System
Task
Route claim to clearinghouse using insurer's payer ID
▶
Suggested Opportunity
Surface the resolved payer ID in the claim submission confirmation so billers can verify routing before the claim leaves Jane.
4
Change Insurer on Existing Policy
Step 4.1
Discover the wrong insurer was used
Biller
Task
Identify that a policy was created under the wrong insurer (via rejection, review, or patient update)
▶
Pain Point
Discovery typically happens only after a claim rejection — not at intake. By the time it's caught, multiple visits may have been billed to the wrong payer, compounding cleanup effort
Feedback"If the mistake isn't caught early, it gets worse — claims go out to the wrong insurer, get denied weeks later, and untangling the mess is a significant amount of work." — Maddie Maerz, Product Brief
Clinic Admin
Task
Navigate to patient profile and locate the incorrect policy
▶
Suggested Opportunity
A "flagged for review" policy state or a filter for policies with recent claim rejections would make it easier to track in-progress corrections across multiple patient profiles.
Step 4.2
Attempt to edit the insurer on the policy
Clinic Admin
Task
Try to change the insurer — field is currently locked in Jane
▶
Pain Point
The insurer field on an existing policy cannot be edited — the only path is archiving the policy and recreating it, which destroys the uploaded insurance card and splits policy history
Feedback"Often clinics setup a policy under the wrong insurer. This adds a lot of duplicate effort because you need to re-create the policy identically except modify which insurer it is created under." — Ethan, Billing Service ride-along
Pain Point
188+ Canny votes confirm this as one of the most-requested fixes in the insurance space — surfaces repeatedly in the Facebook community and support channels
Feedback"This one has strong signal — 188+ Canny votes and it comes up regularly in our Facebook community and beyond." — Maddie Maerz, PM Brief
Opportunity
Active project in #proj-changeinsurer to ship a single guided flow for changing insurer on a policy. Key open questions: which associated claims to update, versioning vs in-place edit, restrictions post-claim submission.
Step 4.3
Workaround: archive & recreate policy
Clinic Admin
Task
Archive incorrect policy, re-enter all details under the correct insurer
▶
Pain Point
Archiving destroys the patient's uploaded insurance card — it must be re-requested or manually re-uploaded, adding friction for both staff and patient
Pain Point
Splitting a policy creates a worse paper trail than updating in-place — the history of what was submitted to which insurer becomes fragmented across two policy records
Community"Does splitting the policy give us a worse paper trail? If we force a new policy after a rejection, we're not really representing what is happening outside of Jane." — Maddie Maerz
Biller
Task
Mark affected claims as Unsubmitted and resubmit to correct insurer
▶
Pain Point
For payer ID changes affecting hundreds of patients, billers face this same cleanup at scale: duplicate insurer in Settings + new policy per patient + mark claims Unsubmitted + resubmit — a full day of work
Community"The key effort here is marking those claims as Unsubmitted, but it's much lighter than rebuilding policies from scratch." — Karen, Billing Service
Opportunity
A bulk "reassign insurer" tool — triggered from the insurer settings page when a payer ID changes — would let clinics do in one operation what currently takes hours across individual patient profiles.
